**Verdict: CONFIRMED with NEW FINDING**

| Unit Type | Economic (sqm) | Socialized (sqm) | Verification |
|---|---|---|---|
| Single-family dwelling | 22 | 18 | Confirmed by S1, S2, S3 |
| Multi-family / condominium | 22 | 18 | Confirmed by S1, S2 |
| BP 220 condominium (all) | 18 | 18 | Confirmed by S1, S2 |

The primary extraction is correct for the 2008 IRR base standards.

**NEW FINDING — Secondary source conflict on two-storey economic housing:**
- Some secondary sources (CDO Homebuilder, Studocu summaries) claim 30 sqm minimum floor area for two-storey economic housing.
- The LawPhil IRR text (S1) does NOT contain any reference to "30 square meters" and states a uniform 22 sqm minimum for all economic housing regardless of number of storeys.
- The 30 sqm figure likely originates from practitioner interpretations or the R-973 (2018) amendment which set 32 sqm GFA as the minimum for the new price ceiling tier, not from the base 2008 IRR.
- **For computation purposes, use 22 sqm (base IRR) unless the project specifically avails of the R-973 price ceiling, in which case 32 sqm GFA applies.**

**NEW FINDING — JMC 2025-001 floor area update (effective December 23, 2025):**
- New minimum floor area for socialized housing raised from 22 sqm to **24 sqm** (for the new price ceiling tiers).
- Price ceiling tiers now differentiate 24-26 sqm (P844,440) from 27+ sqm (P950,000) for horizontal projects.
- This supersedes R-973 for new license-to-sell applications filed after December 23, 2025.
- **The 18 sqm floor area minimum from the base 2008 IRR may now be effectively obsolete for socialized housing under the JMC 2025-001 regime, though it technically remains in the IRR text.**

## 6. Road ROW Standards (Table 5)

**Verdict: CORRECTED — Major errors in primary extraction**

The primary extraction presented a flat table:
- Major Road = 10m
- Minor Road = 8.0m

**This is INCORRECT.** The actual Table 5 is a matrix that varies by project size AND housing type:

### Corrected Table 5: Road Right-of-Way (meters)

| Project Size (ha) | Economic Major | Economic Collector | Economic Minor | Socialized Major | Socialized Collector | Socialized Minor |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 2.5 and below | 8 | -- | 6.5 | 8 | -- | 6.5 |
| Above 2.5-5.0 | 10 | -- | 6.5 | 10 | -- | 6.5 |
| Above 5.0-10 | 10 | 8 | 6.5 | 10 | -- | 6.5 |
| Above 10-15 | 10 | 8 | 6.5 | 10 | 8 | 6.5 |
| Above 15-30 | 12 | 8 | 6.5 | 10 | 8 | 6.5 |
| Above 30 | 15 | 10 | 6.5 | 12 | 10 | 6.5 |

**Additional road types (confirmed by S1):**

| Road Type | ROW | Carriageway | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Motor Court (Cul-de-sac) | 6.0 m | 5.0 m | Max length 60m |
| Alley (economic) | 2.0 m | -- | Both ends connecting to streets; not for property access |
| Alley (socialized) | 3.0 m | -- | |
| Pathwalk (socialized only) | 3.0 m | -- | Max length 60m; pedestrian access to property |

**Specific corrections to primary extraction:**

1. **"Minor Road = 8.0m" is WRONG.** The IRR consistently specifies minor roads at **6.5m** ROW across all project sizes. The 8.0m figure is for collector roads (which only appear in projects above 5.0 ha for economic or above 10 ha for socialized). The primary extraction likely confused collector roads with minor roads.

2. **"Major Road = 10m" is an oversimplification.** Major road ROW ranges from **8m** (for projects <= 2.5 ha) to **15m** (for economic projects > 30 ha). The most common figure is 10m (for mid-size projects), but using it universally would fail compliance for large projects or incorrectly require it for small ones.

3. **"Alley = 2.0-3.0m" needs splitting.** Economic housing alleys = 2.0m; socialized housing alleys = 3.0m (not a range).

4. **"Interior lot access = 3.0m" does not appear as a separate road type in Table 5.** The 3.0m figure corresponds to the pathwalk (socialized) or the interior lot frontage minimum — not a road ROW category.

5. **Collector roads are missing from the primary extraction.** For projects above 5-10 ha, collector roads (8m or 10m ROW) are required and represent an additional road hierarchy level not captured.

6. **Interconnecting road rule:** When a major road serves as an interconnecting road between contiguous projects or phases, the minimum ROW is 10m regardless of project size. (Confirmed by S1)

## 10. Verification of Claimed Conflicts/Uncertainties

### Conflict 1: Corner lot frontage 8m vs 10m
**RESOLVED — 8m is correct.**
- The 2008 IRR Table 8 (confirmed by S1, S3, S5, S7) consistently specifies 8.0m for single detached corner lots.
- The 10m figure is the minimum ROW for interconnecting roads, NOT a lot frontage standard.
- Some sources may also confuse this with PD 957 corner lot frontage (which ranges from 10-12m).

### Conflict 2: Socialized row house lot area 28 sqm vs 32 sqm
**RESOLVED — Both are correct, from different IRR versions.**
- **28 sqm** = 2008 IRR (R-824), Table 7. This is the base standard still in the IRR text.
- **32 sqm** = 2018 amendment (R-973), which introduced new minimum GFA of 32 sqm tied to the new price ceiling tier. Under R-973, new lot configurations were specified (e.g., 4m x 4m per floor for two-storey = 32 sqm GFA on various lot sizes).
- For projects under the base IRR price ceiling (P450,000), 28 sqm remains valid.
- For projects availing of the R-973 (and later JMC 2025-001) price ceiling tiers, the lot size effectively increases.
- **For computation purposes:** The lot area minimum is 28 sqm (base IRR). The floor area minimum may effectively be 32 sqm (R-973) or 24 sqm (JMC 2025-001) depending on which price ceiling regime the project falls under.

### Conflict 3: Density calculation — lots/ha vs DUs/ha
**RESOLVED — The IRR uses "lots/DU per hectare" as the unit.**
- The Table 1 header reads "No. of lots/DU per hectare" — this means lots OR dwelling units per hectare, whichever is applicable.
- For horizontal subdivision projects (single detached, duplex, row house): use number of **lots** per hectare.
- For multi-family / condominium: use number of **dwelling units** per hectare.
- For mixed typology: the IRR does not explicitly resolve this. Practitioner convention is to use total dwelling units (counting each unit in a multi-family building) divided by gross area.

## 11. Regulatory Currency Check

### Is the 2008 IRR (R-824) still the current operative regulation?

**YES, with amendments layered on top.** Timeline:

| Year | Issuance | What Changed |
|---|---|---|
| 2008 | HLURB Board Res. R-824 | Revised IRR of BP 220 (base document, effective Feb 11, 2009) |
| 2009 | HLURB AO 001-09 | Amended Rule II (Sec 5.C.2.b and c, Sec 6.A.2.h, Sec 6.B.1.g, 6.B.2.a-b and c.6, Sec 19 Item j) and deleted Sec 6.A.2.f and Rule V Sec 19 Item I |
| 2018 | HLURB Board Res. R-973 | New tiered price ceiling for socialized housing; new minimum GFA of 32 sqm (28 sqm with loft) under new ceiling; new lot area configurations |
| 2018 | HLURB Board Res. R-974 | Socialized condominium minimum floor area = 22 sqm; new price ceiling range P600K-P750K |
| 2019 | RA 11201 | Created DHSUD from HLURB; all regulatory functions transferred |
| 2025 | JMC 2025-001 (DHSUD/DEPDev) | New socialized housing price ceilings: P844,440 (24-26 sqm) / P950,000 (27+ sqm); new socialized condo ceilings (P1.28M-P1.8M); effective Dec 23, 2025 |

**Key point:** The 2008 IRR remains the base regulatory document. R-973 (2018) and JMC 2025-001 (2025) modify specific provisions (price ceilings, floor area minima tied to price ceiling tiers) but do NOT replace the full IRR.

**No comprehensive new IRR has been issued by DHSUD since 2019.** The DHSUD website (S4) still hosts the 2008 IRR PDF as its primary document.

## 12. Medium-Rise / High-Rise Socialized Housing Condominiums

**NEW FINDING — Limited separate standards exist.**

The base 2008 IRR treats multi-family/condominium as an extension of single-family standards with the following differences:
- Floor area minimum: 22 sqm (economic) / 18 sqm (socialized) per living unit
- Parking: 1 slot per 8 units
- Building separation: per storey-based table (4m to 10m+)
- Setbacks: per Table 11 (storey-based, 2.0m to 5.0m)
- Elevator: required 6+ storeys
- Fire alarm/suppression: required > 15m height
- Stairs/exits/corridors: must conform with National Building Code and Fire Code

R-974 (2018) added socialized condominium-specific rules:
- Minimum floor area: 22 sqm (increased from 18 sqm for socialized condo specifically)
- Price ceilings by location: P600K-P750K (NCR premium)

JMC 2025-001 further updated:
- Socialized condo ceilings: P1.28M-P1.8M depending on floor count and floor area
- NCR/HUC add-on: P50K-P200K additional to ceiling

**No separate structural, fire, or infrastructure standards exist for high-rise socialized housing beyond the general BP 220 IRR provisions + National Building Code compliance.**

## 13. Additional Standards Not in Primary Extraction

### Infrastructure Standards (NEW FINDINGS)

**Drainage:**
- Minimum system: concrete lined canal with load bearing cover
- Minimum drainage pipe diameter: 300mm (RCP)
- Must conform with natural drainage pattern; cannot drain into private lots

- Minimum: septic tank (individual or communal)

- Must conform with Philippine Electrical Code

- Electric bills shared proportionately pre-COC

**Road Pavement:**
- Asphalt: 50mm minimum thickness
- Concrete: 150mm minimum, 20.7 MPa compressive strength at 28 days
- Crown slope: 1.5-9%

### Planning Standards (NEW FINDINGS)

**Road intersection rules:**
- Should intersect at right angles where practicable
- Distance between offset intersections: minimum 20m corner-to-corner
- Road hierarchy must be observed (no major road aligning with alley/pathwalk)

**Lot design:**
- Every lot must have independent access (road, motor court, alley, or pathwalk)
- No lot in hazard zones (erosion, flooding, fault lines, etc.)
- Courts/yards minimum dimension: 2.0m
- Separation walls/fences: maximum 2.0m height

**Easement requirements:**
- Water Code easements on water bodies
- NPC easements on transmission lines
- PHIVOLCS fault trace easements
- Public utility right-of-way easements
