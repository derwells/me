## Specific Verification Items

### Item 1: RPT Rates — max 1% province, max 2% city/Metro Manila

**CONFIRMED** — Section 233 of RA 7160.

- Province: not exceeding 1% of assessed value (S2, S3, S7)
- City or municipality within Metro Manila Area: not exceeding 2% of assessed value (S2, S3, S7)

All sources agree. Note: Quezon City applies differentiated rates — 1.5% for residential, 2% for commercial/industrial/special — both within the statutory maximum (S8).

---

### Item 2: SEF — additional 1% on assessed value

**CONFIRMED** — Section 235 of RA 7160 (S2, S3, S7).

An additional one percent (1%) tax on the assessed value of real property accrues exclusively to the Special Education Fund (SEF).

---

### Item 3: Idle land levy — up to 5% additional on assessed value (Sec. 236)

**CONFIRMED** — Section 236 of RA 7160 (S3, S9).

A province or city, or municipality within Metro Manila, may levy an annual tax on idle lands at a rate not exceeding 5% of the assessed value, in addition to the basic RPT. This is optional (requires an LGU ordinance). Quezon City, for example, imposes 3% on idle lands along national roads and 1% for other idle lands (S8).

---

### Item 4: Assessed Value = FMV x Assessment Level (Section 199)

**CONFIRMED** — Section 199(h) defines "assessed value" as the fair market value of the real property multiplied by the assessment level applicable to its actual use. Section 199(g) defines "assessment level" as the percentage applied to FMV to determine assessed value (S2, S3).

---

### Item 5: RPT = Assessed Value x RPT Rate

**CONFIRMED** — This is the standard formula. RPT = Assessed Value (i.e., FMV x Assessment Level) x Tax Rate (set by LGU ordinance, not exceeding Section 233 caps) (S2, S3).

---

### Item 6: Land and building assessed SEPARATELY then summed

**CONFIRMED** — Section 218 provides separate assessment level schedules for (a) lands, (b) buildings, (c) machinery, and (d) special classes. Each is assessed independently. The total RPT is the sum of taxes on each component (S2, S3). This is demonstrated in the illustrative computation found in S3 where a house-and-lot property has land assessed at 20% and the structure assessed by its FMV bracket, then summed.

---

### Item 7: Actual use determines classification, NOT zoning/title (Section 217)

**CONFIRMED** — Section 217 of RA 7160 provides that real property shall be classified based on its actual use and not on the basis of its ownership, although the title, ownership documents, or zoning may be considered as indicators of actual use (S2, S3). The blog at tanunginmoako also covers Section 217 separately confirming this.

---

### Item 8: LGU ordinance sets actual rates; Section 218 provides MAXIMUMS only

**CONFIRMED** — The statutory text explicitly states "at the rates not exceeding the following" (S1, S2). LGUs may set lower levels via ordinance but cannot exceed the Section 218 ceilings. Example: Quezon City residential RPT rate is 1.5% (below the 2% Metro Manila max) (S8).

---

### Item 9: General revision every 3 years (Section 219)

**CONFIRMED** — Section 219 of RA 7160 mandates that the provincial, city, or municipal assessor shall undertake a general revision of real property assessments every three years (S2, S7).

**Amendment note:** RA 12001 (RPVARA) repeals Section 219 and replaces it with a requirement that LGUs conduct general revisions within two years of RPVARA effectivity and every three years thereafter (S4).

---

### Item 10: RA 12001 (RPVARA) — 6% cap; Section 218 maximums preserved

**CONFIRMED with nuance** — Based on the full text of RA 12001 (S4):

- **Section 218 maximums preserved:** Section 29 of RA 12001 explicitly states: "the maximum assessment levels in Section 218 of RA No. 7160 shall be observed."
- **6% cap text:** "For the first year of effectivity of the approved SMV in accordance to this Act, any increase in real property taxes shall be limited to a maximum of six percent (6%) of the real property taxes assessed on such properties prior to the effectivity of this Act."
- **Key clarification:** The 6% cap applies to the first year of effectivity of the *approved SMV* (not the first year of the law itself). It caps the *increase in RPT*, not the assessment levels.
- **RA 12001 does NOT change the assessment level percentages.** It reforms the valuation framework (single valuation base via Schedule of Market Values per Philippine Valuation Standards) but the Section 218 assessment level ceilings remain intact.
- **Sections repealed:** 199(e), (g), (o); 212; 219 of RA 7160
- **Sections amended (insofar as inconsistent):** 19, 135(a), 138, 201, 218, 220, 472(b)(8) of RA 7160

The primary extraction's claim is **correct but could be more precise**: RA 12001 amends Section 218 "insofar as inconsistent" but explicitly preserves its maximum assessment levels. The amendment is to the valuation framework (how FMV is determined), not to the assessment level percentages themselves.

---

### Item 11: Section 225 depreciation — MACHINERY only, not buildings

**CONFIRMED** — Section 225 of RA 7160 is titled "Depreciation Allowance for Machinery" and applies exclusively to machinery:
- Maximum annual depreciation: 5% of original/replacement/reproduction cost per year
- Minimum residual value: 20% of cost as long as machinery remains useful and in operation
- Building depreciation is NOT covered by Section 225. Building depreciation is handled through the schedule of fair market values and base unit construction costs established by local assessors per BLGF Manual on Real Property Appraisal and Assessment Operations (S2).

---

### Item 12: Mineral land buildings — separate table or commercial/industrial?

**FINDING: No separate graduated table exists for mineral land buildings.**

Section 218(b) provides graduated building assessment tables for four classifications only:
1. Residential
2. Agricultural
3. Commercial/Industrial (combined)
4. Timberland

There is NO separate Section 218(b) sub-table for "mineral" buildings. The DOF LAR 1-92 (S2) confirms this: the four building tables are the only ones provided. For mineral lands, the land itself is assessed at 50% (under Section 218(a)), but buildings on mineral lands would be classified and assessed based on their actual use under Section 217 — meaning they would most likely fall under the commercial/industrial building schedule if used for mining/mineral extraction operations. This is consistent with the general principle that actual use determines classification.

**This is a structural observation, not a correction to the primary extraction.** The primary extraction did not claim a separate mineral building table — it only listed the four building schedules that exist in Section 218(b). The finding here confirms that the four-schedule structure is exhaustive.

**Observation:** Most Metro Manila cities adopt rates at or near the statutory maximum (2%) for commercial/industrial properties. The main variability is in residential RPT rates (some cities use less than 2%) and in idle land levies (most LGUs have not adopted the full 5%).

**Note:** Assessment levels (the Section 218 percentages applied to FMV) are generally adopted at or near the statutory maximums by Metro Manila LGUs. Provincial LGUs are more likely to adopt lower assessment levels. The BLGF maintains a summary of approved SMVs, assessment levels, and tax rates at https://blgf.gov.ph/smv/ — however, detailed per-LGU assessment level schedules require accessing individual LGU ordinances.
