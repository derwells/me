# BP 220 Standards — Analysis

**Wave:** 1 (Source Acquisition)
**Aspect:** bp220-standards
**Date:** 2026-02-26

## Summary

BP 220 (Batas Pambansa Blg. 220, enacted 1982) authorizes DHSUD (formerly HLURB) to set relaxed minimum design standards for economic and socialized housing projects, as distinct from the higher standards under PD 957 (open market housing). The 2008 Revised IRR (HLURB Resolution No. R-824, Series of 2008, effective 11 February 2009) is the currently operative regulation.

**Key distinction from PD 957:** BP 220 projects have smaller minimum lot sizes, floor areas, and road widths — deliberately relaxed to make affordable housing viable. Compliance checking involves a classification step (is the project economic or socialized?), then comparison against the appropriate tier of standards.

---

## Housing Type Classification

| Classification | Target Market | Price Ceiling Basis |
|---|---|---|
| Socialized Housing | Underprivileged and homeless citizens | Per DHSUD JMC (lowest tier) |
| Economic Housing | Average-income earners | Per DHSUD JMC (economic tier) |

*Note: Open market / medium-cost housing falls under PD 957, not BP 220.*

---

## Minimum Lot Area Standards (Section 5, Table 7 — 2008 IRR)

| Housing Type | Economic (sqm) | Socialized (sqm) | PD 957 Reference (sqm) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Single Detached | 72 | 64 | 100 |
| Duplex / Single Attached | 54 | 48 | 75 |
| Row Houses | 36 | 28 | 50 |

**Row house block limit:** Maximum 20 units per block or cluster; no block longer than 100 meters.

**Source:** IRR BP 220 (2008) Sec. 5, Table 7; cross-confirmed via lawphil.net IRR text.

---

## Minimum Floor Area Standards (Section 4 & 6 — 2008 IRR)

| Unit Type | Economic (sqm) | Socialized (sqm) |
|---|---|---|
| Single-family dwelling | 22 | 18 |
| Multi-family / condominium unit | 22 | 18 |
| BP 220 condominium (all tiers) | 18 (minimum) | 18 (minimum) |

**Comparison to PD 957:** PD 957 does not set a statutory minimum floor area for single-family dwellings; minimum derives from National Building Code room size requirements. BP 220 explicitly sets lower floor area minima.

## Minimum Lot Frontage Standards (Section 5, Table 8 — 2008 IRR)

| Lot Type | Economic (m) | Socialized (m) | PD 957 (m) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Single Detached — Corner/Regular | 8.0 | 8.0 | 12.0 (corner) / 10.0 (regular) |
| Single Detached — Interior | 3.0 | 3.0 | 6.0 |
| Duplex / Single Attached | 6.0 | 6.0 | 7.5 |
| Row House | 4.0 | 3.5 | 3.5 |

**Source conflict note:** One secondary source lists 10m for BP 220 single-detached corner lot; the lawphil.net IRR text and VIZCODE summary both say 8m. The 10m figure may reflect a pre-2008 version or confusion with PD 957 corner lot standard. The 2008 IRR (8m) is used here.

## Open Space / Parks & Playgrounds (Section 5, Table 1 — density-based)

For projects ≥ 1 hectare, open space allocation is required as a percentage of **gross project area**, keyed to lot/unit density (lots or DUs per hectare):

| Density (lots/DU per hectare) | Required Open Space (% of gross area) |
|---|---|
| ≤ 150 | 3.5% |
| 151–160 | 4.0% |
| 161–175 | 5.0% |
| 176–200 | 6.0% |
| 201–225 | 7.0% |
| > 225 | 9.0% + 1% per additional 10 lots/DU above 225 |

**Minimum area:** 100 sqm regardless of density tier (implied by IRR).

**PD 957 comparison:** PD 957 requires a fixed 30% non-saleable allocation (roads + parks + facilities), vs. BP 220's density-based open space table which is lower and does not mandate the 30% total non-saleable area.

## Community Facilities Allocation (Section 5, Table 3 — density-based)

| Density (lots/DU per hectare) | Community Facility (% of gross area) |
|---|---|
| ≤ 150 | 1.0% |
| 151–225 | 1.5% |
| > 225 | 2.0% |

**Mandatory facilities by project size:**
- Projects with 1–499 lots: Neighborhood multipurpose center
- Projects with 500+ lots: Additional facilities (convenience centers, schools, tricycle terminals)

## Building Design Standards

### Minimum Ceiling Height
- Habitable rooms: **2.0 m** (measured from finished floor to ceiling)
- Mezzanine floors: **1.8 m** above and below

### Distance Between Buildings (Multi-Family)
| Building Height | Minimum Separation |
|---|---|
| Two-storey | 4.0 m |
| Three to four storey | 6.0 m |
| More than four storey | 10.0 m |
| Blank walls facing | 2.0 m |

### Parking Requirements
- **Rate:** 1 slot per 8 living units (for multi-family / condominium)
- **Slot dimensions:**
  - Perpendicular/diagonal: 2.5 m × 5.0 m
  - Parallel: 2.15 m × 6.0 m
- **Off-site parking:** Allowed within 100 m if part of the project

### Fire Safety
- Firewall required where dwelling abuts property line: masonry ≥150 mm thick, 0.30 m above highest roof point
- Automatic fire alarm/suppression: required for buildings > 15 m height
- Elevators: required for 6 storeys or higher

### Stairways
- Minimum width: 0.60 m
- Maximum riser height: 0.25 m
- Minimum tread depth: 0.20 m
- Headroom clearance: 2.0 m minimum

---

## Water Supply Requirement
- Minimum: **150 liters per capita per day**

---

## Windows
- Habitable rooms: minimum **10% of floor area** of the room
- Bathrooms: minimum **1/20 of floor area**

---

## Construction Quality Requirement
- Building life span: **at least 25 years**, or aligned with loan term

---

## House-to-Lot Price Ratio (BP 220 Duplex/Row House Lots)
- **60/40 rule:** 60% of selling price for the house component, 40% for the lot
- Applies to saleable lots designated as duplex or row house lots that must include a housing component

## Compliance Check Computation Structure

A BP 220 compliance check is a deterministic process:

### Step 1: Project Classification
```
IF selling_price_per_unit ≤ socialized_housing_ceiling → Socialized Housing standards apply
ELSE IF selling_price_per_unit ≤ economic_housing_ceiling → Economic Housing standards apply
ELSE → PD 957 standards apply (not BP 220)
```

### Step 2: Lot Area Check
```
FOR each lot_type IN [single_detached, duplex, row_house]:
  IF lot_area < min_lot_area[housing_type][lot_type] → NON-COMPLIANT
```

### Step 3: Frontage Check
```
FOR each lot_type, corner_classification:
  IF lot_frontage < min_frontage[housing_type][lot_type][corner_class] → NON-COMPLIANT
```

### Step 4: Floor Area Check
```
IF floor_area < min_floor_area[housing_type] → NON-COMPLIANT
```

### Step 5: Open Space Calculation
```
density = total_lots / gross_area_in_hectares
required_open_space_pct = lookup(density, open_space_table)
required_open_space_sqm = required_open_space_pct × gross_area_sqm
IF actual_open_space_sqm < required_open_space_sqm → NON-COMPLIANT
```

### Step 6: Community Facility Check
```
required_cf_pct = lookup(density, community_facility_table)
required_cf_sqm = required_cf_pct × gross_area_sqm
IF actual_cf_sqm < required_cf_sqm → NON-COMPLIANT
```

### Step 7: Road Width Check
```
FOR each road IN project_roads:
  IF road_row < min_row[road_type] → NON-COMPLIANT
```

**Output:** Pass/fail per standard, with shortfall amounts.

## Key Findings for Wave 2 (bp220-lot-compliance)

1. **Fully deterministic**: All BP 220 compliance checks are pure rule lookups and arithmetic — no subjective judgment required.
2. **Two-tiered standards**: Every threshold has an economic and a socialized tier; classification step is prerequisite.
3. **Density-based open space**: Not a fixed percentage — requires computing density first, then looking up the table.
4. **Update frequency**: The 2008 IRR is the current version; DHSUD may update via administrative order. Price ceiling classification is updated separately (JMC 2024-001 / JMC 2025-001).
5. **60/40 ratio rule**: Specific to duplex/row house lots with housing component — often overlooked in practitioner tools.
6. **Branching complexity for Wave 2:**
   - 3 housing type tiers × 3 lot types × 2-3 frontage sub-types = ~18 standard lookup combinations
   - Open space: 6 density bands + marginal formula for >225
   - Community facility: 3 density bands
   - Total: ~30 distinct rule branches in compliance check

## Conflicts / Uncertainties to Flag in Wave 2
- Lot frontage for single-detached corner lots: 8m (IRR text, 2008) vs. 10m (some secondary sources may reflect pre-2008 version or PD 957 confusion)
- Socialized vs. economic minimum lot area for row houses: 28 sqm (lawphil.net IRR) vs. 32 sqm (one comparative table) — the 28 sqm figure from the IRR text is more authoritative
- Density calculation basis: Some sources compute density as lots/ha, others as DUs/ha — for mixed typology projects the correct unit needs clarification
