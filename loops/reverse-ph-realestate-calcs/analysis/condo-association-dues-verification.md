## 1. Monthly Association Dues Formula (HLURB ECR 001-17 §8)

### Claim: Rate Base = Gross Expense / Gross Area (total saleable area)
**CONFIRMED with NUANCE**

Sources:
- [Jur.ph — HLURB ECR 001-17 Summary](https://jur.ph/laws/summary/guidelines-in-the-kinds-of-dues-fees-and-contributions-that-may-be-collected-by-homeowners-associations): "The rate base is the quotient from the division of the gross expense by the gross area to arrive at the cost per square meter."
- [Inquirer Business — Homeowners Associations' Dues](https://business.inquirer.net/230348/homeowners-associations-dues): Confirms gross expense / gross area formula.
- [Studocu — Execom Res No. 001 S2017](https://www.studocu.com/ph/document/university-of-the-philippines-system/constitutional-law/execom-res-no-001-s2017-guidelines-on-hoa-dues/35613817): Confirms the three-component formula (gross expense, gross area, rate base).

**Nuance on "Gross Area":** The claim equates "gross area" with "total saleable area." ECR 001-17 defines it as "total lot and floor area of all members/beneficial users" — this is the aggregate of individual unit lot+floor areas, not necessarily identical to "total saleable area" in developer terminology (which could include unsold inventory). The distinction matters: the denominator includes only the areas attributable to members and beneficial users contributing to the HOA.

### Claim: Monthly Dues per unit = Unit Floor Area x Monthly Rate per sqm
**CONFIRMED**

All three sources above confirm: charges per member/beneficial user = total lot and floor area of that member × rate base.

### Claim: +10% buffer for members, +20% buffer for beneficial users (non-members)
**CONFIRMED**

- [Jur.ph](https://jur.ph/laws/summary/guidelines-in-the-kinds-of-dues-fees-and-contributions-that-may-be-collected-by-homeowners-associations): "an additional 10 percent for members or 20 percent for beneficial users to cover for any shortfall in collections."
- [Inquirer Business](https://business.inquirer.net/230348/homeowners-associations-dues): Confirms 10%/20% buffers.
- ECR 001-17 defines "beneficial user" as homeowner who is not a member, or developer/owner holding properties as inventory for sale.

### Claim: "Gross area" = total lot and floor area of saleable lots/units
**CORRECTED (minor)**

The ECR says "total lot and floor area of all members/beneficial users" — not "saleable lots/units." The distinction: it refers to the aggregate floor+lot area of all persons required to contribute, not the developer's total saleable area. Unsold lots still held by the developer who is a beneficial user would be included, but the framing is contribution-based, not sales-based.

## 3. VAT/Tax Exemption of Association Dues

### Claim: RMC 65-2012 has been invalidated by Supreme Court in BIR v. First E-Bank Tower Condominium Corp. (GR No. 215801, January 15, 2020)
**CONFIRMED**

Sources:
- [LawPhil — G.R. No. 215801](https://lawphil.net/judjuris/juri2020/jan2020/gr_215801_2020.html): Case decided January 15, 2020. Consolidated with G.R. No. 218924. RMC 65-2012 explicitly declared invalid. Ponente: Justice Lazaro-Javier.
- [Supreme Court E-Library](https://elibrary.judiciary.gov.ph/thebookshelf/showdocs/1/66002): Full text confirms invalidation.
- [KPMG Philippines — Special InTAX June 2020](https://kpmg.com/ph/en/home/insights/2020/06/special-intax-june-2020-issue1-volume5.html): Professional advisory confirming the ruling.

### Claim: Association dues are NOT subject to income tax, VAT, or withholding tax per SC ruling
**CONFIRMED**

All three sources above confirm the SC held:
1. Not subject to income tax (dues "do not constitute profit or gain")
2. Not subject to VAT (dues "do not arise from transactions involving the sale, barter, or exchange of goods or property")
3. Not subject to withholding tax ("withholding tax is intended to facilitate the collection of income tax" — since no income tax applies, no withholding tax either)

### Claim: TRAIN Law Section 109(1)(L) independently exempts association dues from VAT
**CORRECTED — Wrong Subsection Letter**

The TRAIN Law (RA 10963) does independently exempt association dues from VAT, but the correct subsection is **Section 109(1)(Y)**, not Section 109(1)(L).

Sources:
- [All About TRAIN Law — Section 34/109 full text](https://allabouttrainlaw.wordpress.com/2018/03/02/section-34-section-109-of-the-nirc-as-amended-is-hereby-further-amended-to-read-as-follows/): "(Y) Association dues, membership fees, and other assessments and charges collected by homeowners associations and condominium corporations."
- [PwC Philippines — Transforming the Tax Landscape of Condominiums](https://www.pwc.com/ph/en/tax/tax-publications/taxwise-or-otherwise/2020-taxwise-or-otherwise/transforming-the-tax-landscape-of-condominiums.html): Confirms the TRAIN Law express exemption.
- [Grant Thornton PH](https://www.grantthornton.com.ph/insights/articles-and-updates1/lets-talk-tax/reconciling-the-condominium-act-and-the-nirc-for-tax-exemption/): Confirms TRAIN Law inclusion.

Section 109(1)(L) actually pertains to **agricultural cooperative transactions**, not association dues.

### Claim: Only commercial income (e.g., leasing to telcos) exceeding ₱3M threshold subject to VAT
**CONFIRMED with NUANCE**

Sources:
- [Respicio — VAT Requirements for Commercial Lease Below Three Million](https://www.respicio.ph/commentaries/vat-requirements-for-commercial-lease-below-three-million-pesos-annual-gross-in-the-philippines): Gross annual sales/receipts under ₱3M exempt from VAT per Section 109(1)(BB); subject to 3% percentage tax instead.
- [Manila Times — Propriety of Imposition of VAT on Condominium Association Dues](https://www.manilatimes.net/2026/01/13/legal-advice/propriety-of-imposition-of-vat-on-condominium-association-dues/2257342): Confirms commercial income treated separately.
- [Acclime PH — VAT in the Philippines](https://philippines.acclime.com/guides/vat/): ₱3M threshold confirmed.

**Nuance:** Below the ₱3M threshold, commercial income is not VAT-free — it is subject to 3% percentage tax. The claim is correct that full 12% VAT only kicks in above ₱3M, but "subject to VAT" should be understood as "subject to 12% VAT registration requirement."

## 4. Special Assessment Approval Requirements

### Claim: Requires dual approval — majority of board AND majority of general membership
**CONFIRMED**

Sources:
- [DHSUD Model By-Laws](https://dhsud.gov.ph/wp-content/uploads/2021/03/2_BY-LAWS-REGULAR.pdf): Board of directors/trustees may assess and collect "as approved by the majority of the members of the board and members of the association."
- [Respicio — Special Assessment Fees in Condominiums](https://www.respicio.ph/commentaries/special-assessment-fees-in-condominiums-legal-requirements-philippines): Confirms board resolution + owner vote requirement.
- [Respicio — Legality of Condominium Special Assessment for Repairs](https://www.lawyer-philippines.com/articles/legality-of-condominium-special-assessment-for-repairs-philippines): Confirms dual approval mechanism.

**Note:** Some by-laws allow the board to impose small special assessments without membership vote if below a specified threshold. The dual approval requirement is the default/standard but can be modified by specific by-laws provisions for minor amounts.

### Claim: Must be placed in separate trust account from regular dues
**CONFIRMED**

- [Respicio — Special Assessment Fees](https://www.respicio.ph/commentaries/special-assessment-fees-in-condominiums-legal-requirements-philippines): "Regular dues and special assessments must be placed in separate trust accounts; commingling is a red flag in every audit."
- Practitioner guides uniformly emphasize account segregation.

### Claim: Non-recurring expenses need board resolution + 3-week notice + 2-week posting + simple majority vote
**PARTIALLY CONFIRMED / PARTIALLY UNVERIFIED**

- Board resolution + notice + majority vote: **CONFIRMED** across multiple sources.
- "3-week notice" and "2-week posting" as specific timeframes: **UNVERIFIED.** No independent source specifies these exact durations. The standard requirement is written notice at least 15 days before the meeting (per RA 9904 IRR and standard by-laws). The specific "3-week/2-week" figures may come from a particular condominium's by-laws rather than a general regulatory mandate.
- Simple majority vote: **CONFIRMED** for most by-laws (50%+1).

**Correction:** The notice period is typically 15 days per RA 9904 IRR, not specifically 3 weeks (21 days). The "2-week posting" is not a separately verified regulatory requirement — it may be a common by-laws provision but is not mandated by statute.

## 5. Assessment Lien (RA 4726 §20)

### Claim: Unpaid dues become lien when management body registers notice with Register of Deeds
**CONFIRMED**

Sources:
- [LawPhil — RA 4726 Full Text](https://lawphil.net/statutes/repacts/ra1966/ra_4726_1966.html): Section 20: "shall be and become a lien upon the condominium assessed when the management body causes a notice of assessment to be registered with the Register of Deeds."
- [ChanRobles — RA 4726](https://chanrobles.com/republicactno4726.html): Same text confirmed.
- [Supreme Court E-Library](https://elibrary.judiciary.gov.ph/thebookshelf/showdocs/2/7243): Same text confirmed.

### Claim: Lien priority superior to all except real property tax liens
**CORRECTED — Overstated Priority**

The actual text of Section 20 states: "Such lien shall be superior to all other liens **registered subsequent to the registration of said notice of assessment** except real property tax liens and except that the declaration of restrictions may provide for the subordination thereof to any other liens and encumbrances."

Key corrections:
1. The lien is only superior to liens registered **after** the assessment notice — not to all liens generally.
2. Pre-existing mortgages and liens registered before the assessment notice maintain their priority.
3. The declaration of restrictions can further subordinate the assessment lien to other specified encumbrances.

The claim overstates the priority by omitting the "registered subsequent" qualifier. A mortgage registered before the assessment notice would still have priority.

Sources:
- [LawPhil — RA 4726](https://lawphil.net/statutes/repacts/ra1966/ra_4726_1966.html): Exact statutory text.
- [ChanRobles — RA 4726](https://chanrobles.com/republicactno4726.html): Confirmed.

### Claim: Enforceable through judicial or extrajudicial foreclosure
**CONFIRMED with CAVEAT (see First Marbella below)**

The statute provides for both options, but the First Marbella case imposes procedural requirements on extrajudicial foreclosure.

### Claim: Per First Marbella Condominium v. Gatmaytan (GR 163196), Section 20 does not ipso facto authorize foreclosure — must follow proper procedures
**CONFIRMED**

Sources:
- [LawPhil — G.R. No. 163196 (July 4, 2008)](https://lawphil.net/judjuris/juri2008/jul2008/gr_163196_2008.html): SC held that "Section 20 does not by itself, ipso facto, authorize judicial or extrajudicial foreclosure." The notice of assessment alone "neither annotation nor law vests it with sufficient authority to foreclose on the property."
- [ASG Law Partners — Condominium Dues and Foreclosure Rights](https://www.asglawpartners.com/civil-law/2008/07/04/condominium-dues-and-foreclosure-rights-understanding-association-powers/): Confirms the ruling.
- [DivinaLaw — Pay Association Dues or Lose Your Condo Unit](https://www.divinalaw.com/dose-of-law/pay-association-dues-or-lose-your-condo-unit/): Confirms that extrajudicial foreclosure requires special authority (typically a power-of-attorney clause in the Master Deed/Declaration of Restrictions), not merely the statutory lien.

The SC clarified that Act No. 3135 (governing extrajudicial foreclosure) requires a "special power of authority to foreclose" — the assessment lien alone is insufficient.

## 7. Dues Increase Approval

### Claim: HSAC regulatory clearance required if increase exceeds 10% (pending proposed increase to 15%)
**CORRECTED — Nuanced**

The 10% threshold exists, but the characterization needs correction:

**What the law actually says (RA 9904 IRR):** "The association dues or fees shall not be increased by more than ten percent (10%) per annum without the approval of the majority of the members." This means increases above 10% require **membership approval**, not specifically "HSAC regulatory clearance."

However, one legal commentary source (Respicio & Co.) states: "regulatory clearance is compulsory whenever the increase exceeds the thresholds set by HLURB/HSAC guidelines" — suggesting that HSAC/DHSUD filing may be required in addition to membership approval. This appears to be a regulatory practice (post-approval filing/monitoring) rather than a pre-clearance requirement.

**On the proposed 15% increase:** One source mentions "A draft HSAC circular—targeted mid-2025—proposes to raise the 'automatic approval' ceiling for regular dues from 10% to 15%." This could not be independently confirmed through a second source and may be speculative or based on informal practitioner knowledge. **Treat with caution.**

Sources:
- [Respicio — Homeowners Association Dues Increase Limits](https://www.respicio.ph/commentaries/homeowners-association-dues-increase-limits-philippines): Confirms 10% cap; requires majority membership vote above that.
- [Respicio — HLURB Approval Requirement for HOA Dues Increase](https://www.lawyer-philippines.com/articles/hlurb-approval-requirement-for-hoa-dues-increase-philippines): "the requirement of HLURB/HSAC approval for increasing HOA dues is more than a procedural nicety—it is a statutory condition."
- [LawPhil — RA 9904](https://lawphil.net/statutes/repacts/ra2010/ra_9904_2010.html): Statute text.

**Correction:** The claim oversimplifies. The primary mechanism is: (a) board proposes increase, (b) if >10%, requires majority membership vote, (c) filing with DHSUD for monitoring. The term "HSAC regulatory clearance" implies a pre-approval gate that is not clearly established in the statute — HSAC is a quasi-judicial body (adjudicating disputes), while DHSUD handles regulatory oversight. The proposed 15% threshold change is UNVERIFIED beyond a single source.

### Claim: Budget and explanatory notes must be posted on bulletin boards and delivered to doorsteps
**CONFIRMED**

- [Respicio — Special Assessment Fees](https://www.respicio.ph/commentaries/special-assessment-fees-in-condominiums-legal-requirements-philippines): "Budget and explanatory notes must be posted on bulletin boards and, under DHSUD rules, delivered to doorsteps or mailboxes."
- [Respicio — Homeowners Association Dues Increase Limits](https://www.respicio.ph/commentaries/homeowners-association-dues-increase-limits-philippines): Confirms written notice at least 15 days before meeting with rationale and financial projections.

## Critical Findings

### HIGH-SEVERITY CORRECTIONS (3):
1. **TRAIN Law subsection is §109(1)(Y), not §109(1)(L).** Section 109(1)(L) covers agricultural cooperative transactions. This is a factual error in the extraction.
2. **Assessment lien priority is overstated.** The lien is only superior to liens registered *subsequent* to the assessment notice — not to "all" liens. Pre-existing mortgages retain priority. The "registered subsequent" qualifier is legally significant.
3. **DHSUD 10% statutory floor for reserve fund is UNVERIFIED.** No DHSUD department order, circular, or statutory provision establishing this floor could be independently located. The claim appears to originate from a single legal commentary (Respicio & Co.) and may be a practitioner interpretation or informal policy rather than a published regulation. This should be flagged as potentially fabricated.

### MEDIUM-SEVERITY ISSUES (3):
4. **"3-week notice + 2-week posting" timeframes** for special assessments could not be verified in any statute or regulation — likely from specific by-laws rather than general law. The standard is 15 days written notice per RA 9904 IRR.
5. **"HSAC regulatory clearance"** mischaracterizes the mechanism. The 10% threshold triggers a membership vote requirement, not HSAC pre-clearance. HSAC is a quasi-judicial adjudicatory body, not a regulatory approval body.
6. **Proposed 15% threshold increase** could only be found in a single source and could not be independently confirmed.

### LOW-SEVERITY ISSUES (2):
7. "Gross area" framing as "total saleable area" is imprecise — ECR 001-17 defines it as total lot+floor area of members/beneficial users.
8. Economy condo dues upper bound of ₱80 understates the range (actual: ₱50-₱100/sqm).
