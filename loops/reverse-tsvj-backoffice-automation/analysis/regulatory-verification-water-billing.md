## Detailed Verification

### Rule 1: No-Markup Rule

**Claim:** Landlords may NOT charge tenants more than the actual per-cubic-meter cost from Maynilad. No admin/service fees allowed on water bills (unlike electricity where a reasonable admin fee is permitted if in the contract).

**Verdict: CONFIRMED**

**Source 1 — Respicio & Co. (legal commentary):**
> "The core principle is simple: the landlord or lessor is not allowed to profit from the resale or distribution of electricity or water. Any amount charged in excess of the actual cost billed by the franchised utility provider is illegal, refundable, and may constitute unjust enrichment or an unfair trade practice."
> — [Landlord Overcharging on Submetered Utilities in Philippines](https://www.respicio.ph/commentaries/landlord-overcharging-on-submetered-utilities-in-philippines)

**Source 2 — Respicio & Co. (separate article on commercial lessors):**
> Electricity: "the rate to end-users shall not exceed the applicable DU tariff...plus only an ERC-approved administrative fee." Water: "the lessor may charge only the exact tariff per cubic metre plus the fixed metering/maintenance charge listed on the utility bill." The distinction is explicit: ERC permits a capped admin fee for electricity (ERC Resolution 21-2010, 9-2013); MWSS permits no such fee for water.
> — [Legal Limits on Utility Charges by Commercial Lessors Philippines](https://www.respicio.ph/commentaries/legal-limits-on-utility-charges-by-commercial-lessors-philippines)

**Source 3 — Lawyer-Philippines.com (Respicio & Co.):**
> "A landlord or building administrator who receives water through a master meter and redistributes it via submeters is legally prohibited from charging tenants an amount higher than what the concessionaire/local water district would have charged the tenants directly had they been individually metered."
> — [Tenant Rights on Water Submeter Billing](https://www.lawyer-philippines.com/articles/tenant-rights-on-water-submeter-billing)

**Electricity vs. Water distinction confirmed:** ERC allows a capped admin fee for electricity sub-metering; MWSS/water regulations do not allow any admin or service fee on water. The rule as stated is accurate.

### Rule 2: Per-Tier Billing Method (Blended Average Prohibited)

**Claim:** Each tenant's individual sub-metered consumption must start at the lowest Maynilad tier bracket. The "blended average" (total bill / total consumption x tenant share) method is prohibited. MWSS RO ordered PHP 87M+ in refunds for overbilling using blended method (2021-2024).

**Verdict: CONFIRMED**

**Source 1 — MWSS IRR No. 2008-02 (official regulation):**
The MWSS Regulatory Office issued IRR No. 2008-02, "Billing Scheme and Rate Classification for High Rise Buildings and Other Multiple Dwellings," which mandates per-tier individual unit billing. The full text PDF is hosted at:
> [IRR No. 2008-02 (PDF)](https://ro.mwss.gov.ph/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/IRR-No-2008-02_-Billing-Scheme-and-Rate-Classification-for-High-Rise-and-Other-Multiple-Dwellings.pdf)

A 2014 presentation updating the IRR is also available:
> [IRR 2008-02 Presentation (PDF)](https://ro.mwss.gov.ph/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/IRR2008-02-Pres-Aug2014.pdf)

**Source 2 — Lawyer-Philippines.com:**
> "The correct method (MWSS-approved) requires that each tenant's first 10 m3 be charged at [the first tier rate], next 10 at [the second tier rate], etc., regardless of total building consumption. Many landlords illegally use the 'pro-rata average rate' method."
> — [Disputing High Water Bills as Tenants](https://www.lawyer-philippines.com/articles/disputing-high-water-bills-as-tenants)

**Source 3 — Respicio & Co. (legal commentary):**
> "In practice, 2023-2025 saw an explosion of successful tenant complaints, with MWSS RO alone ordering more than PHP 87 million in total refunds from 2021-2024."
> — [Excessive Water Bill Charges Complaint Philippines](https://www.respicio.ph/commentaries/excessive-water-bill-charges-complaint-philippines)

**Note on the PHP 87M figure:** This figure appears in legal commentary from Respicio & Co. and is cited as a cumulative total across multiple cases. I could not locate a single MWSS RO press release publishing this exact aggregate figure. However, individual MWSS RO refund orders are documented in news (e.g., PHP 27.477M penalty on Maynilad per [GMA News](https://www.gmanetwork.com/news/money/companies/858436/mwss-orders-maynilad-to-refund-customers-p27-477m-warns-of-possible-supply-deficit/story/), PHP 14M in *Sweet Water HOA v. Maynilad*). The PHP 87M+ figure is plausible as a multi-year aggregate but should be treated as derived from legal commentary rather than a single official document.

### Rule 3: Common Area Water — Separate Line Item

**Claim:** Must appear as a separate line item; cannot be blended into per-cu.m. rate. Options: (a) separate sub-meter for common areas with cost apportioned by floor area or equal division, or (b) fixed peso amount pre-disclosed in lease.

**Verdict: CONFIRMED**

**Source 1 — Respicio & Co. (commercial lessors article):**
> "Common-area consumption (lifts, escalators, facade lighting) must be billed to the lessor's own account, not divided among tenants."
> — [Legal Limits on Utility Charges by Commercial Lessors Philippines](https://www.respicio.ph/commentaries/legal-limits-on-utility-charges-by-commercial-lessors-philippines)

**Source 2 — Lawyer-Philippines.com:**
> "Common-area consumption and line losses (the difference between the master meter and the sum of submeters) should be borne by the lessor/association, not marked up to tenants, unless a clear, fair formula is agreed upon (e.g., capped allocation disclosed in advance)."
> — [Tenant Rights on Water Submeter Billing](https://www.lawyer-philippines.com/articles/tenant-rights-on-water-submeter-billing)

**Source 3 — Respicio & Co. (overcharging commentary):**
> Common-area consumption should "either be: billed under a separate common-area meter and charged to the landlord/condo corp; or pro-rated...only if the lease expressly so provides."
> — [Landlord Overcharging on Submetered Utilities in Philippines](https://www.respicio.ph/commentaries/landlord-overcharging-on-submetered-utilities-in-philippines)

**Nuance:** Sources consistently confirm that common area water cannot be invisibly blended. The default position is that the landlord bears the cost. If tenants are to share it, the allocation formula must be pre-disclosed, agreed upon, and shown as a separate line item. The two options stated in the rule (sub-meter allocation or fixed pre-disclosed amount) are consistent with the sources.

### Rule 4: Environmental Charge 25% (Effective January 2025)

**Claim:** 25% of Basic Charge, effective January 2025, up from 20%.

**Verdict: CONFIRMED**

**Source 1 — Philippine News Agency (PNA):**
> "The Environmental Charge was set to increase from 20 percent to 25 percent starting January 2025, subject to Maynilad's attainment of sewer coverage of 25 percent by the end of 2024."
> — [MWSS approves Manila Water, Maynilad charge hikes](https://www.pna.gov.ph/articles/1188819)

**Source 2 — Maynilad official disclosure (December 2024):**
> "Beginning 1 January 2025, Maynilad implemented the following tariff changes for West Zone customers: ...an increase in the Environmental Charge from 20% to 25% (which is a function of the basic water charge)..."
> — [Maynilad Notice of New Water Rates 2025 (PDF)](https://www.mayniladwater.com.ph/wp-content/uploads/2024/12/Maynilad-Notice-of-New-Water-Rates-2025-17-December-2024.pdf)

**Source 3 — Maynilad Q3 2025 performance report:**
> Revenues grew 9.5%, "primarily reflecting an 8.0% tariff increase implemented in January 2025 and the adjustment in the Environmental Charge from 20% to 25%."
> — [Maynilad reports solid Q3 2025 performance](https://www.mayniladwater.com.ph/maynilad-reports-solid-q3-2025-performance-and-sustained-investments/)

**Additional note for 2026:** Manila Water's Environmental Charge increased from 25% to 30% in January 2026 (per [Business Inquirer](https://business.inquirer.net/564608/higher-water-bills-loom-as-manila-water-maynilad-hike-rates-in-2026)). Maynilad's 2026 EC rate should be verified separately if the system will be used beyond 2025. The TSVJ property is in Las Pinas (Maynilad West Zone), so the 25% rate is correct for 2025 billing periods.

### Rule 6: Billing Statement Requirements

**Claim:** Must include: initial reading, final reading, consumption in cu.m., per-tier rate breakdown, Basic Charge, Environmental Charge (25%), Sewerage Charge (if applicable), MSC, Taxes, total due, and computation showing how tenant's share derives from master bill.

**Verdict: CONFIRMED with nuance**

**Source 1 — Respicio & Co. (billing statement requirements):**
Required elements include: previous and current meter readings with multiplier, consumption volume in cu.m., tariff block/rate classification, basic charge breakdown, environmental charge, FCDA, service charge, previous balance, payments received, arrears, surcharges, government taxes, due date, disconnection schedule, customer hotline information.
> — [Water Billing Statement Requirements in the Philippines](https://www.respicio.ph/commentaries/water-billing-statement-requirements-in-the-philippines)

**Source 2 — Respicio & Co. (overcharging commentary):**
> Landlords must furnish "a computation sheet that shows: (Tenant kWh / Sum of all sub-meter kWh) x (DU total bill) = Tenant Share."
> — [Landlord Overcharging on Submetered Utilities in Philippines](https://www.respicio.ph/commentaries/landlord-overcharging-on-submetered-utilities-in-philippines)

**Source 3 — Lawyer-Philippines.com:**
> "The landlord must issue clear and accurate billing statements for water consumption. This usually involves showing the initial reading and the final reading for the billing period, the rate applied, and the total amount due."
> — [Tenant Rights on Water Submeter Billing](https://www.lawyer-philippines.com/articles/tenant-rights-on-water-submeter-billing)

**Nuance:** The specific requirement for a "per-tier rate breakdown" on the tenant's billing statement (as opposed to just on the Maynilad bill itself) is not explicitly spelled out in the sources as a standalone mandate. The sources confirm that readings, consumption, rate classification, basic charge, surcharges, and computation methodology must be shown. The per-tier breakdown is implicitly required because the computation must be transparent and verifiable, but no source explicitly lists "per-tier rate breakdown" as a named mandatory line item for the landlord's re-bill to tenants. For compliance purposes, including it is best practice and defensible -- just noting that the specific framing as a mandated line item is an inference rather than a direct quote from regulation.

### Rule 7: Copy of Master Bill Within 5 Days

**Claim:** Landlord must provide photocopy or digital copy of Maynilad master bill to tenants within 5 days of receiving it.

**Verdict: CONFIRMED**

**Source 1 — Respicio & Co. (overcharging commentary):**
> "Landlords should give each tenant a photocopy or digital image of the DU/water concession bill within five (5) days of receipt."
> — [Landlord Overcharging on Submetered Utilities in Philippines](https://www.respicio.ph/commentaries/landlord-overcharging-on-submetered-utilities-in-philippines)

**Source 2 — Lawyer-Philippines.com:**
> "The landlord must provide a copy of the official utility bill every month upon request. Failure to do so creates a presumption that the billing is incorrect."
> — [Tenant Rights on Water Submeter Billing](https://www.lawyer-philippines.com/articles/tenant-rights-on-water-submeter-billing)

**Source 3 — Respicio & Co. (separate article):**
> Confirms the 5-day timeline and adds that landlords must also provide "the original utility bill, a computation sheet, and submeter photos (date-stamped) every billing cycle."
> — [Tenant Right to Separate Water Meter and Billing Philippines](https://www.respicio.ph/commentaries/tenant-right-to-separate-water-meter-and-billing-philippines)

**Note:** Source 2 uses "upon request" language rather than an automatic obligation. The 5-day proactive obligation is confirmed in Sources 1 and 3. The most conservative (and defensible) interpretation is the proactive 5-day disclosure.

### Rule 8: Refund Obligation Within One Billing Cycle + 6% p.a. Interest

**Claim:** Over-collections must be refunded within one billing cycle with 6% p.a. interest.

**Verdict: CONFIRMED**

**Source 1 — Respicio & Co. (overcharging commentary):**
> "Over-collections must be refunded within one billing cycle with interest at the legal rate (currently 6% p.a.) starting from the month of over-collection."
> — [Landlord Overcharging on Submetered Utilities in Philippines](https://www.respicio.ph/commentaries/landlord-overcharging-on-submetered-utilities-in-philippines)

**Source 2 — Respicio & Co. (tenant utility bill credits):**
> "Refunds include 6% annual interest if delayed beyond prescribed periods (Civil Code Article 2209)."
> — [Tenant Rights to Utility Bill Credits and Refunds](https://www.respicio.ph/commentaries/tenant-rights-to-utility-bill-credits-and-refunds)

**Source 3 — MWSS RO billing irregularity process:**
> "Refund/credit must reflect within the next billing cycle."
> — Reported in search results from MWSS RO consumer policies at [ro.mwss.gov.ph](https://ro.mwss.gov.ph/consumer-policies-procedures-guidelines/latest-update/)

**Legal basis confirmed:** Civil Code Article 2209 (interest on obligations); BSP Circular 799 (legal interest rate set at 6% p.a. effective July 2013, replacing the prior 12% rate for loans/forbearance and 6% for other obligations -- now unified at 6% under *Nacar v. Gallery Frames*, G.R. No. 189871).

### Rule 9: Sub-Meter PNS/BPS Certified; Maynilad/MWSS Approval Required

**Claim:** Must be PNS/BPS certified and calibrated; installation requires Maynilad/MWSS approval.

**Verdict: CONFIRMED**

**Source 1 — MWSS RO Policies on Water Meters (BOT Resolution No. 39-2000):**
> Official MWSS policy document on water meter standards.
> — [Policies on Water Meters (PDF)](https://ro.mwss.gov.ph/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/Policies-on-Water-Meters.pdf)

**Source 2 — Respicio & Co. / Lawyer-Philippines.com:**
> "Sub-meters must be Philippine National Standards (PNS) / BPS certified."
> — [Tenant Rights on Water Submeter Billing](https://www.lawyer-philippines.com/articles/tenant-rights-on-water-submeter-billing)

**Source 3 — Respicio & Co. (separate commentary):**
> "MWSS guidelines require landlords to apply for sub-meter approvals, and failure to do so can lead to penalties."
> — [Tenant Right to Separate Water Meter and Billing Philippines](https://www.respicio.ph/commentaries/tenant-right-to-separate-water-meter-and-billing-philippines)

**Additional context:**
- NWRB (National Water Resources Board) provides meter testing/calibration services, with Maximum Permissible Error of +/-3% and recommended calibration every 2 years.
- Maynilad's meter lab has PNS ISO/IEC 17025:2005 certification from DTI-PAB ([Maynilad meter laboratory accreditation](https://www.mayniladwater.com.ph/maynilad-meter-laboratory-gets-dti-pab-accreditation/)).
- Under RA 6234, MWSS is the only body vested with the duty to regulate water meter testing and calibration, though in practice this is done by concessionaires under MWSS imprimatur ([Inquirer Opinion](https://opinion.inquirer.net/92168/its-mwss-that-should-test-and-calibrate-water-meters)).

**Nuance:** The "Maynilad/MWSS approval" requirement for sub-meter installation is confirmed in legal commentary and MWSS guidelines. However, the specific approval process details (which office, what forms, timeline) were not found in a single authoritative regulatory document accessible online. The requirement exists, but implementation details should be confirmed directly with the Maynilad Business Area office covering Las Pinas.

### Rule 10: VAT on Water Pass-Through Not VATable

**Claim:** Water utility pass-through billing (pure cost reimbursement) is NOT a VATable sale -- landlord is a conduit. But if landlord adds markup, it becomes a VATable service.

**Verdict: CONFIRMED**

**Source 1 — Respicio & Co. (commercial lessors):**
> "It is important to distinguish between VATable rent and non-VATable pass-through utilities to avoid BIR assessments."
> — [Legal Limits on Utility Charges by Commercial Lessors Philippines](https://www.respicio.ph/commentaries/legal-limits-on-utility-charges-by-commercial-lessors-philippines)

**Source 2 — Lawyer-Philippines.com (withholding tax):**
> "Prudent lessors keep utility billing totally transparent, segregate it from rental income, and reflect only verifiable pass-through amounts in their lease agreements."
> — [Withholding Tax Calculation on Utility Payments Philippines](https://www.lawyer-philippines.com/articles/withholding-tax-calculation-on-utility-payments-philippines)

**Source 3 — BIR RR 16-2005 and BIR RMC 7-94:**
> BIR Revenue Regulations No. 16-2005 governs VAT on lease of property. The general rule is that rental income is VATable, but pure utility cost pass-through (at cost, separately invoiced) is treated as a reimbursement, not gross receipts.
> — [BIR RMC No. 7-94 on VAT on Sale of Water (SC E-Library)](https://elibrary.judiciary.gov.ph/thebookshelf/showdocs/10/39274); [BIR RR 16-2005 (PDF)](https://bir-cdn.bir.gov.ph/BIR/pdf/26116rr16-2005.pdf)

**Key requirements for non-VATable treatment:**
1. Amounts billed at cost with no markup
2. Separately invoiced from rent (one OR for rent, another for utilities)
3. Lease agreement clearly provides for pass-through billing
4. Transparent records maintained

If any markup is added or utilities are bundled into rent, the BIR may treat the entire amount as VATable gross receipts.

## Flags and Caveats

1. **PHP 87M+ refund figure (Rule 2):** This figure comes from legal commentary (Respicio & Co.) as an aggregate across multiple MWSS RO cases from 2021-2024. Individual refund orders are documented in news sources, but a single official MWSS RO publication confirming the aggregate was not located. Treat as a credible estimate from legal practitioners rather than an official statistic.

2. **Per-tier breakdown as billing line item (Rule 6):** The requirement for the per-tier breakdown to appear on the landlord's re-bill to tenants is a best-practice inference from the transparency obligation, not a verbatim regulatory mandate. Including it is strongly advisable but the exact formatting is not prescribed.

3. **"5 days" master bill copy (Rule 7):** One source (Lawyer-Philippines.com) uses "upon request" language rather than a proactive 5-day obligation. The more conservative interpretation (proactive 5-day disclosure) is supported by two other sources and should be followed.

4. **Sub-meter approval process (Rule 9):** The requirement for Maynilad/MWSS approval is confirmed in principle, but the specific procedural steps were not found in publicly accessible documents. Confirm with the local Maynilad Business Area office.

5. **2026 Environmental Charge:** For Maynilad (West Zone), the 25% rate is confirmed for 2025. Manila Water (East Zone) moved to 30% in January 2026. Maynilad's 2026 EC rate should be verified separately when it becomes relevant to billing.

6. **Source concentration:** Legal commentary sources are heavily concentrated in two outlets (respicio.ph and lawyer-philippines.com, both associated with Respicio & Co.). While these are consistent with government sources (MWSS RO, Maynilad official), they represent a single law firm's interpretation. For any rule where only legal commentary (and not a primary regulatory or news source) confirms it, the confidence level is noted as Medium-High rather than High.
