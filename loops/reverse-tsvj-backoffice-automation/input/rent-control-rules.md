# Rent Control Rules — PH Regulatory Reference
*Fetched: 2026-02-25 | Sources: lawphil.net, dhsud.gov.ph, thecorpusjuris.com, respicio.ph*

## Overview

RA 9653 (Rent Control Act of 2009) remains in force as of February 2026 through a chain of NHSB resolutions. The original statutory rent-increase caps expired December 31, 2013, but RA 9653 Section 6 delegates ongoing regulation to the housing board (now DHSUD/NHSB under RA 11201). Extensions have been issued continuously, currently through NHSB Resolution 2024-01 covering 2025–2026.

**RA 9653 covers residential units only.** Commercial leases are governed solely by the Civil Code.

## Part 1: RA 9653 — Rent Control Act of 2009

**Full title:** "An Act Establishing Reforms in the Regulation of Rent of Certain Residential Units, Providing the Mechanisms Therefor and for Other Purposes"

**Primary source:** https://lawphil.net/statutes/repacts/ra2009/ra_9653_2009.html

### Section 5 — Coverage (Rent Brackets)

| Area | Monthly Rent Range Covered |
|---|---|
| NCR and other Highly Urbanized Cities | P1.00 to P10,000.00 |
| All other areas (non-HUC) | P1.00 to P5,000.00 |

**Explicitly excluded:** Motels, hotels, hotel/motel rooms, commercial units, rent-to-own schemes (Sec. 11).

**Las Piñas applicability:** Yes — Las Piñas City is part of Metro Manila (NCR). Coverage threshold: P10,000/month.

### Section 7 — Payment Schedule, Advance Rent, and Security Deposits

**The "1+2 Rule":**
- Rent is due within the first 5 days of each month (unless contract specifies later).
- Maximum advance rent: **1 month**
- Maximum security deposit: **2 months**, held in a bank under the lessor's account name for the entire duration of the lease.
- **Interest on deposit:** Must be returned to the lessee at lease expiration.
- **Forfeiture:** Deposit (plus interest) is forfeited in proportion to damage if the lessee fails to settle rent, utilities, or destroys house components.

### Section 8 — Assignment and Subleasing

Assignment or subleasing (including accepting boarders/bedspaces) **without the written consent of the owner/lessor is prohibited**. This is a stricter overlay on Civil Code Art. 1650 (which permits subleasing unless expressly prohibited).

### Section 9 — Grounds for Judicial Ejectment (Covered Units)

1. **Unauthorized subletting** without written consent.
2. **Arrears for 3 full months** — lessor must allow consignation if refusing payment. Lessee must deposit within 10 days of each month; failure for 3 months = ejectment.
3. **Legitimate owner/family repossession:** Formal written notice **3 months in advance**; lessor must not re-lease to any other person for **1 year** after repossession.
4. **Condemnation order** requiring repairs — evicted lessee has first preference to return at a proportionate new rent.
5. **Lease contract expiration** (but see Section 12 — Art. 1673(1) suspended).

**Consignation options (if lessor refuses payment):**
- Court deposit
- City/municipal treasurer
- Barangay chairman
- Bank in the name of the lessor (with notice to lessor)
Timeline: within 1 month of refusal; then within 10 days each subsequent month.

### Section 10 — Prohibition Against Ejectment by Sale or Mortgage

**Absolute prohibition:** No lessor or successor-in-interest can eject a lessee on grounds that the leased premises have been sold or mortgaged, **regardless of whether the lease or mortgage is registered**.

### Section 12 — Suspension of Art. 1673(1)

Civil Code Art. 1673(1) (period expiry as ejectment ground) is **suspended** for covered residential units during the effectivity of RA 9653. Lessor cannot eject a covered tenant simply because the lease term ended. All other Civil Code lease provisions remain in effect.

### Section 13 — Penalties

- Fine: **P25,000 to P50,000**
- Imprisonment: 1 month + 1 day to 6 months
- Or both
- Filed with Metropolitan or Municipal Trial Courts
- Prescription: 3 years

*Source conflict: Some secondary sources (Respicio.ph) cite P100,000 upper fine. The correct statutory figure per lawphil.net and The Corpus Juris is P50,000.*

## Part 2: NHSB Resolutions — Rate History and Current Rules

**Authority:** RA 9653 Section 6; institutionalized via RA 11201 (DHSUD Act).
**NHSB Policies page:** https://dhsud.gov.ph/laws-and-issuances-2/policies-and-other-advisories/nhsb-policies/

### Rate History Table

| Resolution | Coverage Period | Rate(s) |
|---|---|---|
| NHSB 2022-01 | Jan 1 – Dec 31, 2023 | 2% (P1–P4,999) / 7% (P5,000–P8,999) / 11% (P9,000–P10,000) |
| NHSB 2023-03 | Jan 1 – Dec 31, 2024 | Uniform **4%** |
| NHSB 2024-01 | Jan 1 – Dec 31, 2025 | Uniform **2.3%** |
| NHSB 2024-01 | Jan 1 – Dec 31, 2026 | Uniform **1%** |

### NHSB Resolution 2024-01 — Current (Jan 2025 – Dec 2026)

**Date approved:** December 2024
**Basis:** NEDA recommendation; pursuant to RA 9653

**2025 rules:**
- Max increase: **2.3%** for units ≤ P10,000/month, same tenant as of 2024, continuing/renewing in 2025.
- Units > P10,000/month: exempt from cap.
- New units first leased in 2025: lessor sets rent freely.
- Boarding houses/dormitories: only **one** adjustment allowed within 2025.

**2026 rules:**
- Max increase: **1%** for units ≤ P10,000/month in 2025, same tenant continuing/renewing in 2026.
- Units > P10,000/month in 2025: excluded from 2026 cap.

**Sources:** PNA https://www.pna.gov.ph/articles/1241056 | PIA https://pia.gov.ph/press-release/govt-reduces-hike-in-monthly-rent-for-residential-units/ | Manila Standard | Tribune

## Part 3: Civil Code of the Philippines — Lease Provisions (Arts. 1642–1688)

**Source:** Title VIII, Book IV (RA 386)

### Key Articles for Back-Office Automation

#### Art. 1654 — Lessor Obligations
1. Deliver thing in fit condition for intended use.
2. Make all necessary repairs during lease to keep it suitable.
3. Maintain lessee in peaceful and adequate enjoyment for entire contract duration.

#### Art. 1657 — Lessee Obligations
1. Pay rent per terms stipulated.
2. Use thing as a diligent father of a family, per stipulated purpose.
3. Pay expenses for the deed of lease.

#### Art. 1665 — Return of Property
Lessee must return property as received, except for losses from time, ordinary wear, or inevitable causes.

#### Art. 1670 — Tacit Reconduction (Implied Renewal)

**Trigger:** After lease expiration, lessee continues enjoying the thing for **15 days** with the **acquiescence** (not mere tolerance) of the lessor, and no notice to the contrary has been given by either party.

**Effect:** Implied new lease is created — NOT for the original term, but for the period under Arts. 1682/1687.

**New term length (Art. 1687, urban residential):**
- Annual rent payment → year-to-year
- Monthly rent payment → **month-to-month** (most common case)
- Weekly → week-to-week
- Daily → day-to-day

*Courts may fix a longer term if lessee has occupied for over 1 year (Art. 1687).*

**Other terms of original contract are revived** (except the period, and guarantees — per Art. 1672).

**RA 9653 interaction:** Even after tacit reconduction creates a month-to-month lease, Art. 1673(1) (period expiry ejectment) remains suspended for covered units. So a lessor cannot eject simply because the month ended.

#### Art. 1671 — Holding Over Against Lessor's Objection
If lessee continues after expiry **over the lessor's objection**, lessee is a possessor in bad faith (subject to damages, fruits, etc.).

#### Art. 1673 — Judicial Ejectment Grounds (Civil Code)
1. Period agreed upon or legal period expired. *(Suspended by RA 9653 Sec. 12 for covered units)*
2. Lack of payment of price stipulated.
3. Violation of any conditions agreed upon.
4. Devotion to non-stipulated use causing deterioration; or failure to use as diligent father of a family.

#### Art. 1676 — Sale of Leased Property
- Registered lease: buyer takes subject to lease; cannot terminate.
- Unregistered lease, buyer unaware: buyer may terminate.
- Unregistered lease, buyer with actual knowledge: buyer cannot terminate.
- RA 9653 Sec. 10 overlay (for covered units): sale/mortgage as grounds for ejectment is **absolutely prohibited** regardless of registration.

### Right of First Refusal
**No general statutory right** for residential lessees in the Civil Code. Must be contractually stipulated. Special statutory rights exist only under:
- **PD 1517 (Urban Land Reform Act):** Only in proclaimed Urban Land Reform Zones/Areas for Priority Development.
- **RA 3844:** Agricultural lessees only (not relevant).

## Source Conflicts Documented

1. **Penalty fine upper limit:** Some sources cite P100,000; correct statutory figure is **P50,000** (lawphil.net, The Corpus Juris).
2. **RA 9653 "lapsed":** Some sources claim law lapsed after 2020. Incorrect — NHSB resolutions under RA 9653 Sec. 6 maintain continuous regulatory authority.
3. **Prior rate bracket structure:** Pre-2024, tiered 2%/7%/11% brackets applied. From 2024 onward, a single uniform percentage applies. Current tools/spreadsheets using tiered brackets for 2024+ rent calculations are wrong.
