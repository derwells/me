## PART 1: ELECTRICITY — MERALCO / ERC

#### Distribution Rate True-Up
A **distribution rate true-up adjustment of ₱0.2024/kWh** for residential customers was implemented beginning the April 2025 billing period pursuant to ERC's order dated March 5, 2025. This represents a reduction passed through to customers.

#### Lifeline Rate (Electricity)
- Applies to residential accounts consuming **≤100 kWh/month**
- Up to **5% discount** on their bill — subsidized by all other customers
- Senior citizens with low consumption also qualify
- **NB for sub-metering:** Individual sub-meter tenants consuming ≤100 kWh cannot automatically claim lifeline rates because eligibility attaches to the **Meralco account** (the master meter), not to sub-metered units. The landlord's master meter aggregate typically exceeds 100 kWh, disqualifying the account from the lifeline rate.

### 1.3 Sub-Metering Legal Framework (Electricity)

#### Core Rule: No Profiteering
**Landlords may only charge tenants the actual per-kWh cost billed by Meralco for the tenant's sub-metered consumption.** No markup, surcharge, or profit margin is permitted.

Legal basis:
- **EPIRA (RA 9136), Section 43** — grants the ERC exclusive authority to regulate electricity rates; prohibits non-utility entities from charging rates beyond what ERC allows
- **ERC Resolution No. 12, Series of 2009** — primary regulation governing sub-metering; emphasizes pass-through principle, no markup above DU rates
- **ERC Resolution No. 08, Series of 2022** — updated guidelines on sub-metering; reinforces no-markup rule and billing transparency requirements
- **RA 7394 (Consumer Act), Article 52** — unconscionable or excessive pricing is an unfair trade practice; DTI has jurisdiction
- **Civil Code Arts. 19–21** — prohibits abusive conduct and unjust enrichment

#### Administrative / Service Fees
- Landlords may charge "reasonable, transparent" administrative/maintenance fees for the sub-metering setup, **only if explicitly agreed upon in the rental contract**
- However, **no specific ERC regulation has established a peso per-kWh cap or a fixed maximum** for such fees
- In practice, any fee that results in an effective per-kWh charge materially above Meralco's actual rate is treated as profiteering
- Adding a flat "billing fee" or "convenience fee" to electricity is legally precarious without clear ERC authorization

#### Prohibited Practices
- Charging a fixed monthly flat rate disconnected from actual consumption
- Using the highest tier rate for all tenants when the building's consumption doesn't justify it at the per-tenant level
- Adding administrative or maintenance fees that inflate the per-kWh rate above Meralco's actual published rate
- Using uncalibrated or unsealed sub-meters

#### Billing Method
- Read each tenant's sub-meter at the same time each month (consistent billing period)
- Compute: `Tenant's Consumption (kWh) × Meralco per-kWh rate for the billing month`
- The per-kWh rate is the **total Meralco bill divided by total kWh consumed on the master meter** for that month (this automatically captures the tiered/generation components correctly)
- Provide tenant with: opening reading, closing reading, consumption, rate applied, breakdown of charges, total due

#### Common Area Electricity
- Common area electricity (hallways, lobby, pumps, landscaping lights) is a **landlord cost**
- Options: (a) absorb as operating expense and include in base rent, or (b) apportion at cost among tenants proportionally (by floor area or equally), disclosed as a separate line item
- The total recovery from all tenants combined (unit consumption + common area portion) cannot exceed the Meralco master bill for that period
- Cannot be embedded as a per-kWh markup on unit consumption — must be segregated

#### Tenant Rights and Remedies
- Right to inspect the sub-meter at reasonable times
- Right to request a copy of the Meralco master bill
- Right to contest readings and request meter testing
- **Refund** of over-collected amounts with **6% per annum** interest (solutio indebiti, Civil Code Art. 2154)
- File complaint with: **ERC Consumer Affairs Service (CAS)** (administrative); DTI (consumer protection); Small Claims Court up to ₱1M (no lawyer required); regular courts (if above ₱1M)

#### Landlord Penalties for Overcharging
- ERC administrative fines
- Court-ordered refunds with 6% p.a. interest
- LGU business permit revocation
- Estafa under the Revised Penal Code if fraudulent intent and meter fabrication involved (People v. Isidoro, G.R. No. 188576, Jan. 26, 2016)

#### Supreme Court Precedents
- **Freedom to Build, Inc. v. Court of Appeals (G.R. No. 176143, July 31, 2013)**: Building administrators are mere conduits; profiteering is prohibited
- **Spouses Ong v. Court of Appeals (G.R. No. 148848, Sept. 4, 2009)**: Landlords imposing surcharges commit unjust enrichment
- **People v. Isidoro (G.R. No. 188576, Jan. 26, 2016)**: Estafa conviction affirmed for fabricating readings and collecting grossly excessive electricity charges

#### Rate Classification: Residential vs. Commercial Master Meter
- Landlords with purely residential units typically hold a **residential Meralco account**
- If the building has commercial tenants, the master meter may be classified **commercial/industrial** with different distribution charge rates
- The rate classification doesn't change the sub-metering rules; the pass-through formula (total bill ÷ total kWh) remains the same regardless of account classification

## PART 2: WATER — MAYNILAD / MWSS

### 2.1 Water Utility

**Maynilad Water Services, Inc.** holds the exclusive West Zone concession covering **Las Piñas City**. Maynilad's concession area includes: Manila, Pasay, Parañaque, Caloocan, Muntinlupa, Las Piñas, Valenzuela, Navotas, Malabon, and portions of Makati, Quezon City, and Cavite.

---

### 2.2 Rate Structure

Maynilad uses a **socialized progressive block-rate (tiered) structure**: lower consumption tiers are billed at lower per-cubic-meter rates. Customers are classified by economic activity: **Residential, Semi-Business, Commercial, or Industrial**.

#### All-in Average Rates (2025–2026)

| Period | All-In Average Residential Rate |
|---|---|
| Q4 2024 | ₱58.30/cu.m. |
| Q1 2025 (effective Jan 1, 2025) | ₱65.62/cu.m. (↑₱7.32/cu.m.) |
| Q3 2025 | ₱65.62/cu.m. with FCDA rebate of -₱0.329/cu.m. |
| Q4 2025 (Oct 2025) | Slight increase (~₱0.14/cu.m.) via FCDA adjustment |
| Q1 2026 (effective Jan 1, 2026) | ↑₱2.15/cu.m. further (2.85% Rate Adjustment Limit) |

The **average basic charge** (before surcharges) = **₱51.40/cu.m.** (used as FCDA computation basis in 2025).

#### Bill Components

| Line Item | Description |
|---|---|
| Basic Charge | Progressive tiered rate by consumption (per-tier rates in Maynilad official tariff PDF at ro.mwss.gov.ph) |
| Environmental Charge | **25% of Basic Charge** from Jan 2025 (increased from 20%) — applicable to all customers |
| Sewerage Charge | **20% of Basic Charge** for **Commercial and Industrial** customers connected to sewer line; **NOT applied** to Residential and Semi-Business accounts |
| FCDA (Foreign Currency Differential Adjustment) | Quarterly adjustment; can be + or −. Q3 2025: -₱0.329/cu.m.; Q1 2026: -0.42% of water charge |
| Maintenance Service Charge (MSC) | Fixed monthly service charge |
| Government Taxes | VAT (12%) and applicable local taxes |

#### Lifeline / Discount Programs

- **Regular Lifeline Rate**: 41.3% discount on Basic Charge for accounts consuming **≤10 cu.m./month** — automatically applied by Maynilad
- **Enhanced Lifeline Program (ELP)** (from Jan 2025 expansion): 51.53% discount for ≤10 cu.m. **plus** 40% discount on 11–20 cu.m. for qualified low-income (4Ps/poverty program) households
- **Senior Citizen Discount**: 5% discount on Basic Charge for Residential or Semi-Business registered owner accounts consuming ≤30 cu.m./month; one discount per account; reverts to regular rate in months exceeding 30 cu.m.

#### Customer Classification Implications for the Landlord
- Residential tenants (apartments) → **Residential** account type for their sub-meters (if individually metered) or sub-billing based on Residential rates
- Commercial tenants → **Commercial** classification; pay higher block rates AND Sewerage Charge (if connected to sewer)
- Semi-Business (tenant has small home-based economic activity) → intermediate rate
- The landlord's **master meter** is classified based on the account holder profile; for a corporation with mixed-use property, the master meter classification should be verified with Maynilad — it affects which tier rates appear on the master bill

### 2.3 Sub-Metering Legal Framework (Water)

#### Core Rule: No Markup — Landlord Is a Conduit

**Landlords are strictly prohibited from charging tenants more than the actual per-cubic-meter cost billed by Maynilad for the tenant's actual sub-metered consumption.** The sub-metering arrangement is lawful only as a pure pass-through.

Legal basis:
- **MWSS Charter (RA 6234)** and MWSS Concession Agreements (1997) — Maynilad's regulatory responsibility ends at the master meter; beyond it, the landlord distributes as a "mere conduit" under MWSS Customer Service Regulations
- **MWSS RO Resolution No. 2016-010** — cited in Philippine legal commentary as the primary regulation establishing sub-metering rules for the concession area; requires "at-cost" billing (full text at ro.mwss.gov.ph)
- **MWSS RO Customer Service Regulations** — standards for metering, billing, service connections in multi-unit buildings
- **RA 7394 (Consumer Act), Article 50** — overcharging for utilities = unfair trade practice; DTI jurisdiction
- **RA 7581 (Price Act)** — profiteering on water (a basic necessity) is criminal: fine ₱5,000–₱2,000,000 and/or 5 months–15 years imprisonment
- **RA 9653 (Rent Control Act), Section 7(d)** — bars unreasonable increases on rent-inclusive utility charges
- **PD 856 (Sanitation Code)** — landlord must provide tenants with safe and adequate water supply
- **Civil Code Arts. 1654–1688** — lessor's obligation to ensure lessee can enjoy premises; interpreted to include access to basic utilities

**Important Caveat on Bulk/Subdivision Arrangements**: The MWSS confirmed via an FOI response that it has not issued a specific circular governing billing beyond the master meter in bulk supply arrangements (where Maynilad sells water to a developer/association in bulk). In such arrangements, the developer may set its own tariff absent an MOA restriction. **This exception does NOT apply** to standard landlord-tenant rental arrangements; in direct tenancy contexts, the no-markup rule applies in full.

#### Service Fee / Administrative Charge (Water)
- **Prohibited**: No MWSS circular expressly authorizes an administrative, service, or convenience fee for water billing beyond the actual water cost
- Any additional charge is treated as markup and is illegal
- Even legitimate costs of maintaining sub-meters and reading them cannot be charged as a per-cubic-meter add-on; they may be factored into base rent, but not added to the water bill

#### Correct Per-Tier Billing Method (Critical)

The legally required billing method is:

> Each tenant's individual sub-metered consumption **starts at the lowest tier bracket**, regardless of where the building's aggregate master meter consumption falls.

Example:
- Tenant A uses 8 cu.m. → Bill at first-tier (lowest) block rate for 8 cu.m.
- Tenant B uses 12 cu.m. → First 10 cu.m. at first-tier rate, next 2 cu.m. at second-tier rate

**Prohibited (but common) method**: Dividing total master bill by total building consumption (m³) to get an "average rate per cu.m." and multiplying by each tenant's sub-meter reading. This "pro-rata blended rate" method is illegal because it shifts higher-tier costs caused by one tenant or common areas onto all tenants equally.

The MWSS RO has ordered more than **₱87 million in total refunds** from building administrators and landlords during 2021–2024 for water overbilling.

#### Sub-Meter Installation Requirements
- Must obtain **Maynilad/MWSS approval** before installing sub-meters (specific application process at Maynilad Business Area office covering Las Piñas)
- Sub-meters must be **Philippine National Standards (PNS)/Bureau of Product Standards (BPS) certified**
- Must be **regularly calibrated and sealed**; uncalibrated or tampered meters expose landlord to criminal liability under anti-pilferage laws
- Contact Maynilad hotline **1626** or visit the Business Area office for registration requirements

#### Mandatory Billing Obligations for Sub-Metered Tenants

Each billing statement issued to a tenant must include:
1. Initial meter reading (sub-meter reading, start of period)
2. Final meter reading (sub-meter reading, end of period)
3. Consumption in cubic meters (difference between readings)
4. Per-tier rate breakdown (cu.m. billed at each rate level)
5. Basic Charge, Environmental Charge (25%), Sewerage Charge (if applicable), MSC, Taxes
6. Total amount due
7. Computation showing how tenant's share derives from the master bill

Additional obligations:
- Provide a photocopy or digital copy of the **official Maynilad master bill** to each tenant **within 5 days** of receiving it, or upon request
- Maintain **3 years** of billing and meter reading records
- Tenants have the right to **inspect and photograph** both master meter and sub-meter at any reasonable time
- Over-collections must be **refunded within one billing cycle** with **6% per annum** interest from the date of over-collection

#### Common Area Water Allocation

**Option A (Preferred): Separate Sub-Meter for Common Areas**
Install a dedicated sub-meter for common areas (hallways, gardens, cleaning areas). This meter's reading is tracked separately. The common area cost is apportioned among tenants:
- By floor area ratio: `Tenant's area ÷ Total rentable area × Total common area water cost`
- By equal division per unit if units are similarly sized
- Must appear as a separate line item on each tenant's bill — never blended into the unit per-cubic-meter rate

**Option B: Fixed Peso Amount Pre-Disclosed in Lease**
If separate metering of common areas is not installed, the lease may stipulate a fixed peso amount per month for common area water. Requirements:
- Expressed as an absolute peso amount (not a percentage of anything variable)
- Disclosed in the lease agreement before signing
- Listed as a separate line item on bills

**Key constraint**: The total amount recovered from all tenants combined (unit consumption + common area allocation) must **never exceed** the amount Maynilad billed on the master meter for the same billing period. Any surplus must be refunded.

#### Tenant Rights and Remedies (Water)
- Right to inspect sub-meter and master meter
- Right to receive a copy of the Maynilad bill within 5 days of receipt
- Right to dispute meter readings and request Maynilad meter testing
- Refund of over-collected amounts with 6% p.a. interest
- Prescription period: 4 years (unjust enrichment, Art. 1145) or 10 years (written contract, Art. 1144)

File complaints with:
- **MWSS Regulatory Office** (ro.mwss.gov.ph) — primary regulator for Maynilad concession
- **DTI** — unfair trade practice (Consumer Act)
- **DHSUD** (formerly HLURB) — for condominiums and subdivisions
- **Barangay** — mandatory first step before court action
- **Small Claims Court** — for amounts up to ₱2M (revised 2023 threshold)
- **RA 7581 (Price Act)** complaints — filed with appropriate government agency; criminal penalties apply
