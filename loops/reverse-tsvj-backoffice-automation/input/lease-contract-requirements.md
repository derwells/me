# Lease Contract Requirements — PH Regulatory Reference

*Fetched: 2026-02-25*
*Scope: SEC-registered corporation acting as lessor in Las Piñas; commercial and residential (rent-controlled) tenants*

## Sources
- Civil Code of the Philippines (RA 386), Book IV Title VIII, Articles 1642–1688 (Lease)
- Civil Code Art. 1403 (Statute of Frauds — writing requirement)
- Civil Code Art. 709 (Registry of Property — third-party binding)
- Civil Code Art. 1670 (Tacit reconduction)
- NIRC Section 194 (DST on Leases), as amended by TRAIN Law (RA 10963)
- Revenue Regulations No. 4-2018 (TRAIN DST implementing rules)
- Revised Corporation Code (RA 11232)
- 2004 Rules on Notarial Practice (A.M. No. 02-8-13-SC)
- RA 9653 (Rent Control Act of 2009) — for residential units ≤ PHP 10,000/month in Metro Manila
- Respicio & Co. commentaries; lawphil.net; chanrobles.com

## 1. Writing Requirement

**Civil Code Art. 1403(2)(e):** Leases of real property for **longer than one year** must be in writing (Statute of Frauds). Without a written contract, the lease is unenforceable in court (though valid between the parties if fully executed).

- **Leases ≤ 1 year:** May be oral; still valid and enforceable between parties.
- **Leases > 1 year:** Must be in writing to be enforceable. Common practice is to put ALL leases in writing regardless of term.
- **Writing threshold:** Art. 1403 also covers agreements where the rent is not expressly stated — write it out.

---

## 2. Notarization

**Not strictly required for validity between parties**, but standard practice and required for:
1. Registering with the Registry of Deeds (RD) → to bind third parties
2. BIR DST filing (notary must verify stamps are affixed before signing)
3. Court admissibility without additional proof of authenticity (notarized = public document)
4. Practical enforceability — banks, government agencies, and courts expect notarized leases

**2004 Rules on Notarial Practice requirements:**
- All signatories must appear in person before the notary
- Valid government-issued IDs must be presented
- Sign only in the notary's presence (pre-signed documents are invalid)
- Prepare **at least 3 original copies** (lessor, lessee, notary each keep one)
- DST stamps must be affixed and cancelled **before** the notary signs
- Acknowledgment clause must state date, place, notary name, evidence of identity

**Notarial fees:** PHP 200–500 per document (Supreme Court ceiling: PHP 500 per notarized document, unless local IBP chapter has set a different schedule).

---

## 3. Registration with the Registry of Deeds

**Civil Code Art. 709:** Rights over immovable property not registered in the Registry of Property do not bind third parties.

- **Leases > 1 year:** Should be notarized AND registered with the RD to protect against subsequent purchasers or encumbrances on the property.
- **RD Fee:** ~PHP 1,000 + IT fee for annotation on the owner's title.
- **Not registering** means the lease is **only binding between lessor and lessee** — a subsequent buyer of the property is not obligated to honor an unregistered lease beyond 1 year.
- For a long-term commercial lease, this is a significant protection for the tenant.

## 4. Mandatory and Essential Clauses

### Minimum required for enforceability:
| Clause | Legal Basis | Notes |
|--------|-------------|-------|
| Party identification | Civil Code Art. 1643 | Full legal names, addresses; for corporate lessee/lessor: corporate name, TIN, authorized signatory |
| Property description | Civil Code Art. 1643 | Address, floor area, unit number, TCT/CCT number |
| Term / duration | Civil Code | Fixed start and end dates, or month-to-month with notice period |
| Rental rate | Civil Code Art. 1643 | Monthly amount in PHP; payment due date; mode of payment |
| Security deposit amount | RA 9653 (residential) | Max 2 months for rent-controlled; commercial: as negotiated |
| Advance rent | RA 9653 (residential) | Max 1 month advance for rent-controlled |
| Permitted use | Civil Code | Residential, commercial, or mixed; prohibited activities |
| Subletting/assignment restrictions | Civil Code Art. 1650 | Default: not allowed without lessor consent |

### Strongly recommended additional clauses:
- **Rent escalation schedule** — amount or % increase, trigger date, notice period
- **Repair and maintenance allocation** — structural (lessor) vs. minor (lessee); Art. 1654
- **Utilities** — who pays what; sub-metering rights if applicable
- **Termination and ejectment grounds** — notice periods; grounds for early termination
- **Return of premises condition** — normal wear and tear vs. lessee-caused damage
- **Force majeure** — suspension of rent if property made uninhabitable (Art. 1659)
- **Governing law and venue** — Philippine law; specific RTC/MTC jurisdiction (typically Las Piñas for local disputes)
- **Insurance** — fire, liability coverage responsibilities
- **Board resolution reference** — for corporate lessors: reference to board resolution number authorizing the lease

### For rent-controlled residential tenants (RA 9653 mandatory rules embedded in contract):
- Cannot increase rent beyond NHSB-allowed percentage during covered period
- Cannot evict except on specific grounds (non-payment 3+ months, need for owner's use, subleasing without consent, etc.)
- Grace period: 1 month for non-payment before eviction proceedings begin (no self-help eviction)
- Must return security deposit within 30 days of lease end (less deductions)

## 5. Corporate Lessor Requirements (SEC-Registered Corporation)

When a **corporation acts as lessor**, additional requirements apply under the Revised Corporation Code (RA 11232):

### A. Board Resolution
- The corporation's Board of Directors must pass a **Board Resolution** authorizing the execution of a specific lease contract.
- The resolution should specify:
  - Property being leased
  - Lessee name
  - Lease term and rental rate
  - Name and position of the **authorized signatory** (e.g., President, Corporate Treasurer, or designated officer)
  - Authority to negotiate, execute, sign, and deliver all related documents
- Adopted at a meeting where a quorum is present; recorded in **Minutes of the Board Meeting**.

### B. Secretary's Certificate
- The **Corporate Secretary** issues a Secretary's Certificate certifying:
  - The board passed the resolution (verbatim or substantially quoted)
  - The secretary has custody of corporate records
  - The resolution "has not been amended or rescinded and is still in full force and effect"
- Must be **notarized** for use in third-party transactions (banks, BIR, RD, lessees)
- The Secretary's Certificate accompanies the lease contract as an attachment

### C. Who Can Sign Without Board Resolution
Under jurisprudence (Revised Corporation Code + Supreme Court decisions), certain officers have **implied authority** to bind the corporation without a specific board resolution:
- President / CEO
- General Manager (if authorized in bylaws)
- Chairperson of the Board
- BUT: For real property leases (high-value, multi-year), explicit board authorization is best practice regardless

### D. Corporate Seal
- Optional (no longer mandatory under RA 11232), but commonly still affixed to formal contracts
- Corporate name, SEC registration number

### E. Corporate Bylaws Check
- Verify that the corporation's **secondary purposes** include leasing / real estate income
- Some corporate charters require board approval for leases above a certain value or duration threshold
- Board can delegate to officers (e.g., President can sign leases ≤ 1 year without board resolution if bylaws permit)

## 6. DST on Lease Contracts

**NIRC Section 194** (as amended by TRAIN Law, RA 10963, effective January 1, 2018):

### Rate
| Portion of Annual Rent | DST |
|------------------------|-----|
| First PHP 2,000 | PHP 3.00 |
| Every PHP 1,000 (or fractional part) in excess of PHP 2,000 | PHP 1.00 additional |

**Applied per year of the lease term.**

### Formula
```
Annual DST = PHP 3.00 + CEILING((Annual Rent − PHP 2,000) / PHP 1,000) × PHP 1.00
Total DST = Annual DST × Number of Years
```

### Example
- Monthly rent: PHP 15,000 → Annual rent: PHP 180,000
- Annual DST = PHP 3.00 + ((180,000 − 2,000) / 1,000) × PHP 1.00
- Annual DST = PHP 3.00 + 178 × PHP 1.00 = **PHP 181.00**
- For a 2-year lease: PHP 181.00 × 2 = **PHP 362.00**

### Who Pays
- **Primarily the lessor** (as the party executing the document)
- Contract may stipulate that lessee bears DST (common in commercial leases)
- If lessee is a corporation, they may include DST in their monthly remittances

### Filing Deadline
- Within **5 days after the close of the month** when the taxable document was made
- BIR Form 2000 (DST Return) or via eBIRForms/eFPS
- Payment via authorized agent banks or eFPS

### Renewals
- Each renewal or extension = **new contract = new DST obligation**
- Even a simple addendum extending the term triggers DST on the extended period
- A tacit reconduction (month-to-month holdover) may NOT trigger DST if no new document is signed

### Advance Rent in DST Base
- If advance rent is paid (e.g., 1 month advance + 2 months deposit), the advance rent portion is included in the DST computation base.
- Security deposits are **not** included (they are refundable, not consideration for use).

### Exemptions (Section 199 NIRC)
- Leases with government entities
- Diplomatic missions
- Agrarian reform leases (RA 6657)
- **No exemption** for residential vs. commercial or low-rent leases — DST applies to all written leases

## 7. Tacit Reconduction (Art. 1670)

If a fixed-term lease expires and the lessee **continues in possession for 15 days** with the lessor's knowledge and without objection:
- The lease is **deemed renewed** (tacit reconduction)
- Period of renewal: if the original lease was for a definite term, the new term is month-to-month (or year-to-year for agricultural leases)
- The same conditions apply, EXCEPT that the **term becomes indefinite**
- **Risk for lessor**: unintentional renewal creates ejectment complications

**Implication for back-office system**: The system should alert when a lease is within 30–60 days of expiry and flag if the tenant is still occupying after the end date without a signed renewal.

---

## 8. Lease Renewal vs. Extension vs. New Contract

| Situation | DST | Board Res. Needed? | Notes |
|-----------|-----|-------------------|-------|
| New lease contract | Yes — full DST for new term | Yes | Fresh document, full DST |
| Extension addendum (same terms, longer period) | Yes — DST on the extension period only | Recommended | Addendum, not a new contract |
| Renewal (new contract after expiry) | Yes — full DST for new term | Yes | Treated as new contract for DST |
| Tacit reconduction (no new document) | No — no document signed | No | But creates ambiguity; document it anyway |
| Month-to-month holdover (written) | Yes — DST on the written holdover period | Recommended | Even short holdovers, if in writing |

---

## 9. Key Risk Areas for Back-Office System

1. **DST tracking**: Every new or renewed lease must trigger DST computation and BIR Form 2000 filing within 5 days of month-end.
2. **Board resolution expiry**: If the Secretary's Certificate references a specific board resolution, confirm the resolution is still in force (not superseded by a later board action).
3. **Tacit reconduction alerts**: Flag leases approaching expiry; track whether tenant has vacated or is in holdover.
4. **Registration status**: Track which leases > 1 year have been registered with the RD (for title protection).
5. **DST on renewal vs. extension**: Distinguish in the system — both trigger DST but the base computation differs.
6. **Corporate lessee requirements**: If the lessee is a corporation, request their Board Resolution + Secretary's Certificate authorizing the lease before execution.
7. **Rent-controlled protections embedded in contract**: System should flag rent-controlled units and prevent generating contracts with clauses that violate RA 9653 (e.g., oversize deposits, illegal escalation rates).
