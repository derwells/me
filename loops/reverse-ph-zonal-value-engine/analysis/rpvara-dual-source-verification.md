## Claim 1: Section 29(b) Transitional Rule — Three-Way Max

**Claim:** During the RPVARA transition period, the CIR shall adopt the existing SMVs, zonal values, or actual price — whichever is higher — for computing internal revenue tax. BIR ZV continues to be used alongside LGU SMV and SP in a three-way max.

**Verdict: CONFIRMED**

**Source 1 — RA 12001 Full Text (SC E-Library):**
Section 29(b): "In case the SMVs are not yet available or updated, the Commissioner of Internal Revenue shall adopt the existing SMVs, zonal values, or the actual price in consideration as stated in real property transaction documents, whichever is higher, for purposes of computing any internal revenue tax."
URL: https://elibrary.judiciary.gov.ph/thebookshelf/showdocs/2/97502

**Source 2 — SRMO Law (Siguion Reyna, Montecillo & Ongsiako):**
"While the SMVs are still being developed, the BIR shall adopt the existing SMVs, zonal values or the actual price in consideration as stated in real property transaction documents, whichever is higher, for purposes of computing any internal revenue tax."
URL: https://srmo-law.com/legal-updates/understanding-republic-act-no-12001-or-the-real-property-valuation-and-assessment-reform-act-of-2024-rpvara/

**Source 3 — BDB Law:**
"This means that during the transition period (while the new SMVs are not yet prepared and approved), the old 'whichever is higher' rule among zonal values, existing SMVs, and actual consideration effectively continues to apply."
URL: https://www.bdblaw.com.ph/index.php/newsroom/articles/tax-law-for-business/1173-real-property-valuation-law

**Source 4 — PwC Philippines:**
Confirms the continuation of the three-source "whichever is higher" rule during the transition.
URL: https://www.pwc.com/ph/en/tax/tax-publications/taxwise-or-otherwise/2024/modernizing-property-valuation.html

**Nuances/Corrections:** None. The claim accurately describes Section 29(b). The characterization as a "three-way max" is a correct simplification.

## Claim 2: Section 18(c) Post-Transition Rule — Two-Way Max

**Claim:** After BLGF-approved SMVs are available, the CIR uses the SMV or the actual gross selling price — whichever is higher — making it a two-way max (BIR ZV no longer relevant).

**Verdict: CONFIRMED**

**Source 1 — RA 12001 Full Text (SC E-Library):**
Section 18(c): "The Commissioner of Internal Revenue shall use the SMV or the actual gross selling price in consideration, as stated in real property transaction documents, whichever is higher, in computing any internal revenue tax."
URL: https://elibrary.judiciary.gov.ph/thebookshelf/showdocs/2/97502

**Source 2 — BDB Law:**
"It must be emphasized that the CIR may only use the SMV or the gross selling price, whichever is higher, in computing internal revenue tax. This repeals previous rules on property valuation referring to the BIR's SZV, the LGU's SMV, and the recent selling price of property sold within an area, whichever is higher."
URL: https://www.bdblaw.com.ph/index.php/newsroom/articles/tax-law-for-business/1173-real-property-valuation-law

**Source 3 — Cruz Marcelo:**
Confirms that the CIR no longer independently determines property values and must use the BLGF-approved SMV.
URL: https://cruzmarcelo.com/modernizing-and-standardizing-the-valuation-of-real-property-in-the-philippines-the-real-property-valuation-and-assessment-reform-act/

**Source 4 — Deloitte SEA:**
Confirms that with RPVARA, BIR zonal values are no longer determined by the CIR for IRT purposes; replaced by SMV.
URL: https://www.deloitte.com/southeast-asia/en/services/tax/perspectives/rpvara.html

**Nuances/Corrections:** None. The characterization of a "two-way max" is accurate. Multiple law firm analyses confirm the CIR loses the power to independently determine zonal values.

## Claim 3: Section 31 Saving Clause — No Automatic Expiration

**Claim:** Existing BIR zonal values continue in force and effect until repealed, superseded, modified, revised, set aside, or replaced by BLGF-approved SMVs. There is no automatic expiration.

**Verdict: CONFIRMED**

**Source 1 — RA 12001 Full Text (SC E-Library):**
Section 31: "The zonal values, as determined by the BIR and approved by the Secretary of Finance for internal revenue tax purposes... shall continue to be in force and effect until repealed, superseded, modified, revised, set aside, or replaced in accordance with Section 16 of this Act, which shall be within two (2) years upon the effectivity of this Act."
URL: https://elibrary.judiciary.gov.ph/thebookshelf/showdocs/2/97502

**Source 2 — SRMO Law:**
"During the pendency of the preparation and approval of the PVS and SMVs, the zonal values and fair market values as determined by the CIR and local assessors, respectively, will continue to be in force and effect until repealed or replaced by the approved SMVs."
URL: https://srmo-law.com/legal-updates/understanding-republic-act-no-12001-or-the-real-property-valuation-and-assessment-reform-act-of-2024-rpvara/

**Source 3 — PwC Philippines:**
"The SZVs and existing SMVs will continue to be in force until repealed by BLGF-approved SMVs within two years from the effectivity of the RPVARA."
URL: https://www.pwc.com/ph/en/tax/tax-publications/taxwise-or-otherwise/2024/modernizing-property-valuation.html

**Nuances/Corrections:** Minor nuance — the "within two years" clause in Section 31 is grammatically ambiguous. It says zonal values "shall continue to be in force and effect until... replaced in accordance with Section 16 of this Act, **which shall be within two (2) years upon the effectivity of this Act**." The "which shall be within two years" appears to modify the replacement action (i.e., the replacement should happen within 2 years), NOT the continuation (i.e., the zonal values do not auto-expire at the 2-year mark). All law firm analyses confirm this reading — zonal values persist until affirmatively replaced. The claim's statement that "there is no automatic expiration" is correct.

**Claim:** Issued April 7, 2025, this BIR RMC circularized the IRR of RA 12001 (RPVARA). This is the primary BIR issuance acknowledging the transition.

"RMC No. 30-2025 circularizes the Implementing Rules and Regulations (IRR) for RA No. 12001, 'An Act Instituting Reforms in Real Property Valuation and Assessment in the Philippines...'"

**Source 2 — BIR CDN (RMC 31-2025 Digest, corroborating date):**
RMC 31-2025 was issued April 7, 2025 (same date as RMC 30-2025), confirming the April 7 date cluster. RMC 31-2025 "clarifies the provisions on the applicable taxes due on sale of property considered as ordinary assets of the seller."

## Claim 5: LGU SMV Format Differs from BIR ZV

**Claim:** BIR zonal values use Street/Vicinity/Classification Code (RR, CR, RC, CC, etc.)/ZV per sqm (Annex C format from RMO 31-2019). LGU SMVs use land classification by class (1st class, 2nd class, etc.) with values per sqm, plus separate building and machinery schedules. These are fundamentally different data structures.

**Verdict: CONFIRMED**

**Source 1 — RMO 31-2019 (BIR, via BLGF-hosted PDF):**
Annex C is the format of the Schedule of Recommended Zonal Values. Key columns: Street/Subdivision, Vicinity, Classification Code (Annex B codes: RR, CR, RC, CC, I, A1-A50, etc.), ZV/sqm. Covers land only. Organized by RDO → Province → Municipality → Barangay.
URL: https://blgf.gov.ph/wp-content/uploads/2015/09/BIR-RMO_No.-31-2019-SMV.pdf

**Source 2 — Province of Isabela Proposed SMV 2024 (GR Form No. 1):**
LGU SMV uses classifications: R-1 (1st Class Residential), R-2 (2nd Class Residential), R-3 (3rd Class Residential), C-1 (1st Class Commercial), C-2 (2nd Class Commercial), I-1 (1st Class Industrial), etc. Includes separate schedules for: (a) land by location and class, (b) buildings by construction type with depreciation, (c) machinery by acquisition/replacement cost.
URL: https://provinceofisabela.gov.ph/wp-content/uploads/2025/05/2024-SFMV-Province-of-Isabela-34-Municipalities-and-2-Component-Cities.pdf

**Source 3 — Davao City Proposed SMV 2026:**
Confirms the LGU format includes: Schedule of Base Unit Market Values for Residential/Commercial/Industrial Lands by Location/Vicinity with sub-classification criteria, Schedule of Market Values for Agricultural Lands by Class, Schedule of Base Unit Construction Cost for Buildings, and depreciation schedules.
URL: https://davaocity.gov.ph/wp-content/uploads/2025/11/Proposed-SMV-2026.pdf

**Source 4 — BLGF Manual on Real Property Appraisal and Assessment Operations:**
Prescribes GR Form No. 1 (Schedule of Fair Market Values) covering land, buildings, and machinery in a standardized format organized by LGU classification tiers (R-1 through R-n, C-1 through C-n, etc.), distinct from BIR Annex C.
URL: https://blgf.gov.ph/wp-content/uploads/2015/08/ManualRPAandAO.pdf

**Nuances/Corrections:** The claim is accurate in substance. One minor clarification: some LGUs use broader ranges than stated. The claim mentions "1st class, 2nd class, etc." while the actual codes are typically R-1 through R-5 (or up to R-12 in larger cities like Davao), C-1 through C-8, and I-1 through I-3+. The number of tiers varies by LGU. Also, BIR Annex C covers land only, while LGU SMV covers land + buildings + machinery — this difference is correctly captured in the claim.

**Claim:** The RPVARA SMV approval process (assessor prepares -> BLGF Regional Office reviews -> BLGF Head endorses -> DOF Secretary certifies) has not completed for any LGU. LGUs like Davao City, Bataan, and Tacurong City have published PROPOSED SMVs but none are BLGF-certified.

**Verdict: PARTIALLY CONFIRMED — High confidence but not definitively provable from public sources**

**Source 1 — Davao City Government Website:**
Published "Proposed Schedule of Market Values (SMV) for 2026" in November 2025. The word "Proposed" in the title confirms it has not been approved. Davao realtors are actively resisting the proposal.
URL: https://davaocity.gov.ph/proposed-schedule-of-market-values-smv-for-2026/

**Source 2 — Bataan Provincial Government:**
Published "2026 Proposed Schedule of Market Values (SMV) for the Province of Bataan." Again explicitly "Proposed," not certified.
URL: https://bataan.gov.ph/2026-proposed-schedule-of-market-values-smv-for-the-province-of-bataan/

**Source 3 — Tacurong City Government:**
Published "Proposed Schedule of Market Values of Real Properties in Tacurong City" — explicitly in proposal stage.
URL: https://tacurong.gov.ph/wp-content/uploads/2026/01/Proposed-Schedule-of-Market-Values.pdf

**Source 4 — BLGF SMV Summary Page (blgf.gov.ph/smv/):**
The BLGF maintains a summary of approved SMVs. While the page was not directly accessible for verification (HTTP 403), no search results indicate any LGU has completed the full RPVARA Section 16 certification process. The BLGF is still conducting "Skills Enhancement Training" sessions for assessors as of 2025, suggesting the approval pipeline has not yet produced any certifications.
URL: https://blgf.gov.ph/smv/

**Source 5 — Mindanao Times:**
Reports Davao realtors are resisting the proposed 2026 SMV, citing 300-1,181% increases. This opposition further indicates the Davao SMV is far from certification.
URL: https://www.mindanaotimes.com.ph/davao-realtors-resist-proposed-market-value-schedule-for-2026/

**Nuances/Corrections:** The claim is rated "Partially Confirmed" only because a definitive negative is hard to prove — it is possible that a small LGU somewhere has quietly completed the process without public reporting. However, all available evidence strongly supports the claim. No search result, law firm analysis, or government publication identifies any LGU that has received BLGF certification under the new RPVARA process as of March 2026. The approval pipeline description (assessor -> BLGF RO -> BLGF Head -> DOF Secretary) is accurately stated per Section 16 of RA 12001 and the IRR.

## Claim 7: July 5, 2026 Is the Statutory Deadline

**Claim:** LGUs must update SMVs within 2 years from RPVARA effectivity (July 5, 2024). The deadline is July 5, 2026.

**Verdict: CONFIRMED (with minor date nuance)**

**Source 1 — RA 12001 Full Text:**
Section 19: "All provincial assessors, together with municipal assessors and city assessors, shall update their respective SMVs within two (2) years from the effectivity of this Act."
Section 33 (Effectivity): Takes effect 15 days after complete publication. Published June 18, 2024.
URL: https://elibrary.judiciary.gov.ph/thebookshelf/showdocs/2/97502

**Source 2 — CHLP Realty:**
"The amnesty covers penalties, surcharges, and interest on unpaid taxes due before July 5, 2024, and runs for two years — from July 5, 2024 until July 5, 2026."
URL: https://www.chlprealty.com/post/real-property-tax-2025-real-property-valuation-and-assessment-reform-act-rpvara-latest-updates-on

**Source 3 — Lamudi:**
"Local assessors of LGUs are tasked to update their respective Schedule of Market Values within two years from the law's passing."
URL: https://www.lamudi.com.ph/journal/real-property-valuation-assessment-reform-act/

**Source 4 — PwC Philippines:**
"The preparation and approval of the PVS and SMVs should take place within two (2) years from the effectivity of RPVARA."
URL: https://www.pwc.com/ph/en/tax/tax-publications/taxwise-or-otherwise/2024/modernizing-property-valuation.html

## Claim 8: Historical LGU Compliance Is ~37-42%

**Claim:** Only about 37-42% of LGUs complied with the LGC-mandated 3-year SMV revision cycle before RPVARA.

**Verdict: PARTIALLY CONFIRMED — Range is reasonable but sources vary**

**Source 1 — BLGF Training Session Announcement:**
"Since 1993 up to the present, LGUs' compliance with the LGC requirement has continued to decline, with only 37% of LGUs and 60% of Revenue District Offices (RDOs) having complied with the updating of SMVs."
URL: https://blgf.gov.ph/blgf-advocates-for-sustainable-valuation-system-in-the-country-through-skill-enhancement-training-on-the-updating-of-schedule-of-market-values-smv-and-conduct-of-general-revision-gr-of-real-proper/

**Source 2 — PIDS Study (2021):**
"The 7th general revision in 2012 posted an overall compliance ratio (CR) of 31%, an increase from the 6th general revision with a CR of 24%." Also notes that "73 out of 224 or 32% revised their SMVs for the period 2010 to 2013" — specifically "32 out of 80 provinces and 41 out of 143 cities."
URL: https://serp-p.pids.gov.ph/publication/public/view?slug=local-government-units-compliance-in-the-mandated-revision-of-the-schedule-of-market-values-smvs

**Source 3 — PwC Philippines (citing 2021 data):**
"In 2021, data shows that 60% of SMVs and 38% of SZVs are outdated, or were not revised in the last three years."
URL: https://www.pwc.com/ph/en/tax/tax-publications/taxwise-or-otherwise/2024/modernizing-property-valuation.html

**Source 4 — CPBRD (Congressional Policy and Budget Research Department):**
Published "Schedule of Market Value Profile of LGUs" research noting widespread non-compliance.
URL: https://cpbrd.congress.gov.ph/ff2022-60-schedule-of-market-value-profile-of-lgus/

**Nuances/Corrections:** The claimed range of "37-42%" is reasonable but somewhat generous. The data is heterogeneous:
- BLGF's own figure: **37%** compliance (most frequently cited)
- PIDS study (2012 revision cycle): **31-32%** compliance
- PwC (interpreting 2021 data): **40%** compliant (if 60% are outdated)
- The "42%" figure appears to come from some analyses referencing cities+provinces combined at certain points in time

The most defensible figure is **37%** (BLGF's own cited number). The range "37-42%" could be replaced with "approximately 37%" or "31-40% depending on the measurement year and methodology." The claim is not wrong but the upper bound of 42% is less well-sourced than the lower bound. **No source contradicts the general finding of very low compliance.**

## Claim 9: RPVARA Transition Will Create Per-LGU Regime Fragmentation

**Claim:** Since LGUs will certify SMVs at different times, some properties will be under the new Section 18(c) regime while others remain under the transitional Section 29(b) regime. The engine must detect which regime applies per LGU.

**Verdict: CONFIRMED**

**Source 1 — RA 12001 Structure:**
Section 29(b) applies "in case the SMVs are not yet available or updated" — this is a per-LGU condition. Section 18(c) applies when approved SMVs exist. Since certification happens per-LGU, the regime is necessarily per-LGU.
URL: https://elibrary.judiciary.gov.ph/thebookshelf/showdocs/2/97502

**Source 2 — Deloitte SEA:**
Notes that different LGUs will transition at different times, creating a staggered implementation.
URL: https://www.deloitte.com/southeast-asia/en/services/tax/perspectives/rpvara.html

**Source 3 — FileDocsPhil:**
"This situation may deem the RPVARA the new law on real property taxes in lieu of the LGC. If the LGC provisions on real property taxation are not repealed, the new law may govern simultaneously with the LGC, which may lead to legal conflicts."
URL: https://www.filedocsphil.com/rpvara-law-for-real-property-tax-in-the-philippines/

**Source 4 — Senate SEPO Policy Brief:**
Notes that there are "1,715 LGUs" each requiring separate SMV preparation and certification, inherently creating a phased rollout.
URL: https://legacy.senate.gov.ph/publications/SEPO/PB_Real%20Property%20Valuation_02April2024.pdf

**Nuances/Corrections:** None. This is a logical consequence of the statutory design that all sources confirm. The claim is correct that per-LGU regime detection is a necessary engine requirement.

## Claim 12: 6% RPT Cap Applies Only to RPT in First Year, Not to Internal Revenue Taxes

**Claim:** The 6% RPT cap applies only in the first year of new SMV effectivity, per Section 29(c). This limits real property tax increases but does NOT affect internal revenue taxes (CGT, DST, CWT) — those use the full SMV value from day one.

**Verdict: CONFIRMED**

**Source 1 — RA 12001 Full Text (SC E-Library):**
Section 29(c): "For the first year of effectivity of the approved SMV in accordance to this Act, any increase in **real property taxes** shall be limited to a maximum of six percent (6%) of the **real property taxes** assessed on such properties prior to the effectivity of this Act."
Note: The provision explicitly and repeatedly references "real property taxes" — not "internal revenue taxes" or taxes generally.
Section 18(c) separately provides: "The Commissioner of Internal Revenue shall use the SMV or the actual gross selling price... whichever is higher, in computing any internal revenue tax." No cap is mentioned for IRT.
URL: https://elibrary.judiciary.gov.ph/thebookshelf/showdocs/2/97502

**Source 2 — BDB Law:**
Distinguishes between the 6% RPT cap and the CIR's use of SMV for IRT, treating them as separate provisions with different mechanics.
URL: https://www.bdblaw.com.ph/index.php/newsroom/articles/tax-law-for-business/1173-real-property-valuation-law

**Source 3 — Grant Thornton Philippines:**
"In order to avoid potential issues arising from the sudden spike in the value of real properties, the RPVARA imposes a cap of six percent (6%) on the increase of **real property taxes** in the first year of effectivity of the SMV." Separately discusses IRT computation without any cap reference.
URL: https://www.grantthornton.com.ph/insights/articles-and-updates1/lets-talk-tax/the-reformation-of-real-property-valuation-insights-on-the-rpvara-and-its-implementing-rules-and-regulations/

**Source 4 — Inquirer Business:**
"The RPVARA provides that for the first year of its effectivity, any increase in **real property taxes** is capped at 6 percent."
URL: https://business.inquirer.net/464433/are-real-property-taxes-set-to-increase

**Nuances/Corrections:** The claim is accurate. One important nuance: the 6% cap in Section 29(c) says "six percent (6%) of the real property taxes assessed on such properties **prior to the effectivity of this Act**." This means the cap baseline is the RPT assessed before RA 12001 took effect (July 2024), not the RPT from the immediately preceding year. This is a subtle but important distinction for calculation purposes. The claim's statement that "internal revenue taxes (CGT, DST, CWT) use the full SMV value from day one" is correct — no cap provision applies to Section 18(c) IRT computation.

## Flags and Disagreements

**No sources disagree on any claim.** All 12 claims are consistent with the statutory text and multiple independent analyses. Two claims are rated "Partially Confirmed" due to:

1. **Claim 6** — Proving a negative (no LGU has been certified) requires exhaustive checking of all ~1,715 LGUs, which no public source provides. However, the strong circumstantial evidence (all named LGUs still at "Proposed" stage, zero completions mentioned in any source, BLGF still conducting basic training) supports the claim.
