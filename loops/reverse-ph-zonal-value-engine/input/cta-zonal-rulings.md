## Case 1: CIR v. Aquafresh Seafoods Inc. — G.R. No. 170389 (SC, Oct 20, 2010)
**Origin:** CTA E.B. No. 77 → CTA Case No. 6409 (CTA First Division)
**Subject:** Classification dispute — residential vs. commercial zonal value
**Location:** Barrio Banica, Roxas City (RDO 72)

### Facts
- June 7, 1999: Aquafresh Seafoods Inc. sold two parcels (TCT T-21799, T-21804) in Barrio Banica to Philips Seafoods Inc. for PHP 3,100,000
- Aquafresh paid CGT of PHP 186,000 and DST of PHP 46,500 based on residential classification (RR) at PHP 650/sqm (1995 Revised Zonal Values)
- BIR's Special Investigation Division (SID) reclassified properties as commercial (CR) at PHP 2,000/sqm
- September 15, 2000: Regional Director Leonardo Q. Sacamos (Revenue Region Iloilo) issued deficiency assessments:
  - CGT: PHP 1,372,171.46
  - DST: PHP 356,267.62
- BIR evidence: Buildings, water tanks, enclosures, machineries on premises → "used in pursuit of commercial activities"
- BIR alleged connivance with City Assessor's Office to change classification prior to sale

### Holdings (CTA En Banc, affirmed by SC)
1. **Published classification prevails over actual use**: "Even assuming arguendo that the subject properties were used for commercial purposes, the same remains to be residential for zonal value purposes." All of Barrio Banica was classified as residential under the 1995 Revised Zonal Values.
2. **Predominant use rule is limited**: The rule of assigning zonal values based on "predominant use of property" only applies "when the real property is located in an area or zone where the properties are NOT YET classified and their respective zonal valuation are NOT YET determined." If classification already exists, BIR has no discretion to override it.
3. **Mandatory consultation for reclassification**: Section 6(E) NIRC requires "consultation with competent appraisers both from the private and public sectors" for any change to zonal values. The BIR's unilateral reclassification via SID investigation bypassed this requirement.
4. **Fraud must be proven**: BIR's allegation of connivance with City Assessor was rejected — "allegations of fraud, being a serious charge, must be supported by clear and convincing evidence."
5. **1995 zonal values were properly drafted**: The committee included BIR personnel, DOF representatives, NTRC, Institute of Philippine Real Estate Appraisers, and Philippine Association of Realtors Board.

### Key Amounts
- Residential (RR) zonal value: PHP 650/sqm
- Commercial (CR) zonal value claimed by BIR: PHP 2,000/sqm
- Delta: 3.08x higher assessment

**Source:** https://lawphil.net/judjuris/juri2010/oct2010/gr_170389_2010.html

## Case 2: Spouses Emiliano v. CIR — CTA E.B. No. 1103 (2015)
**Subject:** Due process in zonal value determination; absence of published zonal value

### Holding
- CGT cannot be based on BIR's unilaterally adopted valuation when no published zonal value exists for the property
- Due process in fixing zonal values is essential — BIR must follow statutory procedures under Section 6(E) NIRC
- The BIR cannot substitute its own valuation without going through the STCRPV → TCRPV → ECRPV → DOF Secretary pipeline

**Source:** Referenced in Respicio & Co. commentary and NTRC materials

## Case 3: CIR v. Heirs of Gamboa — CTA Case No. 9720 (2020)
**Subject:** Fallback valuation when no zonal value exists

### Holding
- In the absence of a published zonal value, the BIR cannot simply adopt a bank's appraised value
- BIR must follow Section 6(F) appraisal procedures for independent valuation
- The ruling reinforces that the BIR's valuation authority is constrained by procedural requirements

**Source:** Referenced in Respicio & Co. and legal commentary compilations

## Case 4: CTA Case No. 9234 (CTA Second Division)
**Subject:** Missing classification in Department Order; fallback zonal value
**Location:** Cabuyao area (Laguna)

### Facts
- Zonal values per square meter for General Purpose Interior lots under DO 33-05:
  - Barangay Baclaran: PHP 825/sqm
  - Banay-banay: PHP 360/sqm
  - Banlic: PHP 365/sqm
- Petitioner argued that since "General Purpose Interior" lots were not prescribed in DO 50-2000, an alternative basis should apply

### Significance
- Illustrates the classification gap problem: what happens when a property type/classification doesn't appear in the current Department Order
- Highlights the need for a fallback mechanism when the published schedule has gaps

## Case 5: CIR v. Estate of Benigno Toda Jr. — G.R. No. 147188 (SC, Sep 14, 2004)
**Subject:** FMV determination principles

### Holding
- "FMV should be based on willing buyer-willing seller transactions, not arbitrary impositions"

**Subject:** Evidentiary requirements for BIR valuations

### Holding
- "BIR valuations must be supported by evidence, not presumption"

**Subject:** Private appraisal vs. zonal value in expropriation

- Zonal value is not determinative for just compensation

## Expropriation Cases (Supreme Court — Not CTA, but relevant to ZV authority)

### Republic v. Spouses Legaspi — G.R. No. 221995
- "Zonal valuation, which is merely one of the indices of the fair market value of real estate, cannot be the sole basis for the determination of just compensation"
- Zonal value is the basis for PROVISIONAL compensation under RA 10752, but NOT for final just compensation

### Republic v. Estrella Decena — G.R. No. 212786
- "BIR zonal valuation cannot be taken into consideration as the same is always relatively less than the fair market value"

### Republic v. Spouses Goloyuco — G.R. No. 222551
- "Zonal valuation, although one of the indices of fair market value, cannot, by itself, be the sole basis of just compensation"

### DPWH v. Spouses Mirandilla
- DPWH offered PHP 2,300/sqm based on zonal value
- RTC awarded PHP 15,000/sqm as just compensation (6.5x zonal value)

### Secretary of DPWH v. Spouses Tecson — G.R. No. 179334 (2015)
- Zonal values are not conclusive; courts must consider actual use and market data

## Administrative Issuances (Related to Dispute Resolution)

### RMC 115-2020 (October 21, 2020)
- Certificate of Zonal Values shall only be issued by the RDO having jurisdiction over property location
- Certifications for ONETT (Estate Tax, Donor's Tax, CGT) computation no longer required from taxpayer
- BIR processing offices must access zonal values from BIR website
- Revenue Officer generates screen printout as supporting document
- Property owners may still voluntarily obtain certificates for non-BIR transactions

### BIR Ruling No. OT-028-2024
- Clarified CWT and DST computation basis for installment sales
- Use highest of zonal value and market value per tax declaration at time of AGREEMENT (not closing)
- Per Section 3(J) of RR No. 17-2003

### Revenue Regulations No. 18-2019
- Taxpayers may request review of zonal value if believed to be inaccurate
- Must provide evidence (independent appraisals)

### RR No. 6-01
- Mandates public hearings at RDO level with LGU officials, real estate groups, stakeholders
- Publication: 2 newspapers of general circulation, once/week for 2 consecutive weeks
- Posted at RDO, City/Municipal Hall, barangay halls
- Draft elevated: RDO → Regional Director → BIR National Office → CIR approval
- Effective: 15 days after last newspaper publication (unless later date specified)

## Emerging Developments

- **BIR Zonal Valuation Information System (ZVIS)**: Pilot in RDO 39 (South QC) links GIS parcels to zonal rates
- **Executive Order No. 25 (2023)**: Directed BIR to accelerate nationwide revision of zonal values, especially bringing agricultural land values closer to market reality
- **BIR Digital Transformation Roadmap (2024)**: Projects Geo-Mapping of Zonal Values for online verification by lot/block — anticipated to reduce disputes
- **RPVARA (RA 12001, June 2024)**: CIR will no longer have power to determine zonal values; replaced by unified SMV approved by DOF. 2-year transition period for preparation and approval of PVS and SMVs. During transition, existing zonal values remain in force.
- **Senate Bill 2048**: Proposes harmonizing BIR zonal and LGU SMV cycles to unified 3-year timetable with sanctions for non-compliance
- **DOF RREPI Studies**: Automatic zonal adjustments tied to PSA residential real-estate price index to reduce political friction
