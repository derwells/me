## Republic Act No. 12001 — Key Provisions (Extracted from SC E-Library & LawPhil)

### Section 3 — Declaration of Policy
(b) Adopt market value as the single real property valuation base for the assessment of real property-related taxes in the country, and for the valuation of real property for various transactions by all government agencies.

### Section 4 — Definitions (Selected)
- **(l) Market value** — the estimated amount for which a real property shall exchange on the valuation date between a willing buyer and a willing seller in an arm's length transaction after proper marketing wherein the parties had each acted knowledgeably, prudently, and without compulsion.
- **(o) Philippine Valuation Standards (PVS)** — the latest national standards designed for use in the Philippines based upon International Valuation Standards, duly approved by the Secretary of Finance.
- **(p) Schedule of Market Values (SMV)** — a table of base unit market value for all kinds of real property, whether taxable or exempt, located within an LGU, prepared by assessors pursuant to existing laws, rules and regulations. For properties not included in the SMV, the same shall be appraised at its current market value, and shall be assessed for taxation purposes by applying the prescribed assessment level based on its actual use.
- **(Note: SZV / Schedule of Zonal Values is not separately defined in RA 12001 — it is subsumed under the transition provisions)**

### Section 5 — BLGF as Primary Agency
BLGF is the primary agency to lead the implementation. Powers include: develop, adopt, and maintain the PVS, regulations, and specifications for real property appraisal used for taxation.

### Section 13 — Philippine Valuation Standards (PVS)
BLGF shall develop, adopt, maintain and implement uniform valuation standards to be used by all appraisers and assessors in LGUs. PVS shall conform with international valuation standards and principles. PVS reviewed every 3 years or as often as necessary.

### Section 14 — Valuation of Real Property
All real properties, whether taxable or exempt, shall be valued or appraised based on prevailing market values in the locality where the property is situated.

### Section 15 — Preparation of SMV
Provincial assessors, together with municipal assessors, city assessors (including the lone municipal assessor within Metropolitan Manila Area and the assessor of PHIVIDEC Industrial Authority) shall prepare the SMVs for the different classes of real property situated within their respective LGUs, pursuant to the PVS and other rules, regulations and specifications set by the DOF.

### Section 16 — Review and Certification of SMV
The proposed SMV shall be submitted to the BLGF Regional Office for review. If compliant with PVS, endorsed to BLGF head → certified by Secretary of Finance. If non-compliant, remanded for revision.

### Section 17 — Effectivity of SMV
The approved SMV shall take effect fifteen (15) days after its complete publication in a newspaper of general circulation and posting in at least three (3) conspicuous public places in the LGU.

### Section 18 — Use of Schedule of Market Values
The approved SMV shall be used as basis for:
**(a)** General revision of the assessment and property classification by the local assessor, and in the adjustment of assessment level and tax rates by the Sanggunian;
**(b)** Determination of market value for local transfer tax, sand and gravel tax, community tax, and other fees and charges;
**(c)** The Commissioner of Internal Revenue shall use the SMV or the actual gross selling price in consideration, as stated in real property transaction documents, **whichever is higher**, in computing any internal revenue tax;
**(d)** Real property appraisals and other related purposes of all government agencies, instrumentalities, and GOCCs;
**(e)** [Other purposes as determined by the Secretary of Finance]

**Critical change:** Pre-RPVARA, NIRC Section 6(E) gave the CIR power to determine zonal values. Section 18(a)(3) of RPVARA removes this power — the CIR no longer independently determines property values. The CIR uses the BLGF-approved SMV.

### Section 19 — Updating of SMV
All provincial assessors, together with municipal assessors and city assessors, shall update their respective SMVs **within two (2) years from the effectivity** of this Act. LGUs shall update SMVs and conduct general revision of property assessments and classifications **every three (3) years thereafter**. BLGF shall prepare the schedule for updating SMVs in all provinces and cities. Preparation of SMVs shall be completed **within twelve (12) months** following BLGF notice.

### Section 22 — Real Property Information System (RPIS)
BLGF shall develop and maintain an up-to-date electronic database of:
- Sale, exchange, lease, mortgage, donation, transfer and all other real property transactions and declarations
- Cost of construction or renovation of buildings and structures
- Prices of plant, machinery, and equipment

Updated database available at no cost to all LGUs and national government agencies, including CIR. Private sector access subject to BLGF guidelines. Subject to Data Privacy Act (RA 10173).

### Section 24 — Transmission of Real Property Transactions Data
Upon RPIS operationalization, the following must electronically transmit to BLGF every quarter:
- Registers of Deeds
- BIR
- Notaries public
- Officials issuing building permits
- Geodetic engineers conducting surveys

### Section 26 — Penal Provisions
Fine equivalent to 1-6 months basic salary, or suspension from government service for up to 1 year, or both. Violations include failure to provide data, non-compliance with valuation standards, failure to prepare/submit SMVs within required periods.

### Section 29 — Transitory Guidelines (CRITICAL)

**(a)** LGUs already updating SMVs may continue under Sections 15, 16, 17, and 19, with proper notification and coordination with BLGF.

**(b)** **In case the SMVs are not yet available or updated, the Commissioner of Internal Revenue shall adopt the existing SMVs, zonal values, or the actual price in consideration as stated in real property transaction documents, whichever is higher, for purposes of computing any internal revenue tax.**

**(c)** For the **first year** of effectivity of the approved SMV, any increase in real property taxes shall be limited to a maximum of **six percent (6%)** of the taxes assessed prior to the effectivity of this Act.

**(d)** LGUs may enact an ordinance to impose a cap on real property tax increases for subsequent years.

**(e)** All provinces, cities, and municipalities shall automate their real property tax administration operations. BLGF + DICT shall ensure full automation within 2 years from effectivity.

### Section 30 — Tax Amnesty
Amnesty on penalties, surcharges, and interest from all unpaid real property taxes (including Special Education Fund, idle land tax, other special levy taxes) prior to effectivity. Available for 2 years from effectivity (through July 5, 2026). Options: one-time payment or installment. Excluded: properties sold at public auction, under compromise agreements, or with pending court cases.

### Section 31 — Saving Clause
"The zonal values, as determined by the BIR and approved by the Secretary of Finance for internal revenue tax purposes, together with [assessors]... shall **continue to be in force and effect** until repealed, superseded, modified, revised, set aside, or replaced in accordance with Section 16 of this Act, which shall be within two (2) years upon the effectivity of this Act: Provided, That the PHIVIDEC Industrial Authority... shall continue to levy, assess, and collect real property taxes on real properties within its jurisdiction."

### Section 32 — Repealing Clause
All laws, decrees, executive orders, presidential proclamations, rules and regulations and issuances, or parts thereof, inconsistent with this Act are hereby repealed, amended or modified accordingly.

**Key repeals (implied, not explicitly enumerated):**
- NIRC Section 6(E) power of CIR to prescribe zonal values — effectively superseded
- Previous multi-source "whichever is higher" rule among BIR ZV, LGU FMV, and selling price — replaced by SMV-based rule
- RMO 31-2019 committee structure (STCRPV/TCRPV/ECRPV) — superseded by BLGF RPVS

### Section 33 — Effectivity
Takes effect fifteen (15) days after its complete publication in the Official Gazette or in a newspaper of general circulation. **Published June 18, 2024 → Effective July 3-5, 2024** (sources vary by 2 days; most cite July 5, 2024).

## BLGF MC 001-2025 — IRR of RA 12001

**Signed:** December 10, 2024 by Secretary of Finance
**Published:** December 27, 2024 in newspaper of general circulation
**Effective:** January 11, 2025 (15 days after publication)

### IRR Chapter Structure (relevant chapters)
- **Chapter 3** — Preparation of SMV
  - Section 23: Notice and Responsibility to Prepare
  - Section 24: Compliance with PVS
  - Section 26: Publication of Proposed SMVs (at least 2 weeks prior to public consultations)
  - Section 27: Public Consultations and Hearings (2 mandatory)
  - Section 28: Submission of Proposed SMV
  - Section 30: Review and Certification of Proposed SMV
- **Chapter 6** — Uses of the SMV
- **Chapter 7** — Updating of SMV
  - First updating upon effectivity (2-year window)
  - Regular updating every 3 years
- **Chapter on RPIS** — Sections 46-48 of IRR
  - Section 46: Development of RPIS
  - Section 47: Duty of Register of Deeds to supply assessors with real property transactions data
  - Section 48: Transmission of real property transactions data to BLGF

### SMV Preparation Process (from IRR)
1. BLGF issues notice to all local assessors to submit proposed SMVs
2. Local assessors prepare SMVs per PVS within 12 months of notice
3. Publication at least 2 weeks prior to public consultations
4. 2 mandatory public consultations conducted within 60 days before submission
5. Submission to BLGF Regional Office for review
6. BLGF Regional Office endorses to BLGF Head
7. BLGF Head reviews for PVS compliance
8. If compliant → endorsed to Secretary of Finance for certification
9. If non-compliant → remanded to assessors for revision
10. Certified SMV published → effective 15 days after complete publication

## Pre-RPVARA vs Post-RPVARA Tax Base Comparison

| Aspect | Pre-RPVARA | Transition (now-~2027) | Post-RPVARA |
|--------|-----------|----------------------|-------------|
| **Valuation authority** | CIR (BIR) via NIRC Sec 6(E) | Both BIR ZV and LGU FMV remain | BLGF under DOF |
| **Valuation base** | 3 sources: BIR ZV, LGU FMV, SP → highest wins | 3 sources: existing SMV, ZV, SP → highest wins (Sec 29) | Single SMV by BLGF |
| **Tax base rule (IRTs)** | max(SP, ZV, AFMV) | max(SP, ZV, existing SMV) per Sec 29 | max(SP, SMV) per Sec 18(c) |
| **Update frequency** | 3-5 years (38% outdated) | Existing schedules frozen until replaced | 3 years mandatory |
| **Approval chain** | STCRPV→TCRPV→ECRPV→DOF | Both old and new chains active | Local assessor→BLGF RO→BLGF Head→DOF Sec |
| **RPT cap** | None | 6% first year of new SMV | LGU may enact subsequent caps |
| **Classification system** | BIR Annex B codes (RMO 31-2019) | Existing codes continue | PVS-based classification |

## Key Legal Provisions Affecting Zonal Value Engine Design

### 1. Three-Way Max During Transition (Section 29(b))
During the transition period (July 2024 through ~mid-2026 when BLGF-approved SMVs roll out), the CIR uses:
```
tax_base = max(actual_price, existing_smv, zonal_value)
```
This means the engine must continue to serve BIR zonal values throughout this period.

### 2. Two-Way Max Post-Transition (Section 18(c))
After BLGF-approved SMVs are available:
```
tax_base = max(actual_price, smv)
```
BIR zonal values are no longer relevant.

### 3. Phased Rollout Creates Dual-Source Period
Not all 124 RDOs / ~1,700 LGUs will transition simultaneously. For any given property:
- If the LGU has a BLGF-approved SMV → use Section 18(c) rule
- If the LGU does NOT yet have a BLGF-approved SMV → use Section 29(b) rule
- The engine must detect which regime applies per-property

### 4. BIR Zonal Values Continue In Force (Section 31)
Even after RPVARA, existing BIR zonal values are legally valid until explicitly replaced. There is no automatic expiration — they continue until a BLGF-approved SMV supersedes them for that jurisdiction.

### 5. No BIR RMC Yet on RPVARA Implementation
As of the search date (March 2026), no specific BIR Revenue Memorandum Circular has been issued addressing how BIR offices should operationally handle the transition. The existing RMO 31-2019 and RMC 115-2020 remain the operative BIR issuances for zonal values.

### 6. RPIS Will Centralize Data Eventually
Section 22/24 provisions for the RPIS will eventually create a centralized database that makes property valuation data publicly accessible. However, the RPIS is not yet operational, and the timeline for its development is uncertain.
